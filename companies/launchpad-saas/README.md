# Launchpad SaaS

A prebuilt AI B2B SaaS startup for Ever Works. Hand it a rough idea — "teams struggle with X" — and the org runs it through discovery, ships the thinnest testable feature slice, builds the self-serve growth loop that brings users to their first win, and converts the accounts that need a human into retained, expanding revenue. It grows product-led: the product is the primary engine, and sales and success amplify it rather than replace it.

## Org structure

**CEO & Founder — Priya Malhotra** (root): reframes every idea as a bet with one owner, holds the company roadmap in Now / Next / Later, and owns the final call when teams contend for the same engineering week.

### Product team (manager: Head of Product)
- **Theo Brandt — Head of Product**: decomposes bets into scoped Tasks and is the single review gate against each definition of done.
- **Amara Okoye — Product Manager**: writes the PRD that turns a fuzzy bet into a testable slice with one metric attached.
- **Noah Feld — Product Designer (UX)**: designs the flow with the first-win activation path built in, across all states.

### Growth team (manager: Head of Growth)
- **Sofia Marin — Head of Growth**: owns the acquisition→activation→retention→referral loop, fixing the leakiest stage first.
- **Jonah Pierce — Content Marketer**: problem-first content that brings the right people in and primes them to sign up.
- **Mina Halvorsen — Lifecycle Marketer**: behavior-triggered activation, retention, and resurrection sequences inside the product.

### Revenue team (manager: Head of Sales)
- **Darius Webb — Head of Sales**: discovery-first selling to product-qualified accounts; qualifies hard, disqualifies fast.
- **Lena Castillo — Head of Customer Success**: onboards, protects time-to-first-win, watches account health, and finds honest expansion.

Two leads report to the CEO outside the three teams' management lines: the **Engineering Lead — Ravi Chandra**, who owns velocity, reliability, and the safe release path and builds what Product ships; and the sales/success revenue line above. This keeps engineering a company-wide function rather than a single team's resource.

## Skills

- `prd-writing` — turn a funded bet into the thinnest testable slice with one success metric.
- `product-led-growth-loop` — map the loop, find the leakiest stage, run one experiment against it.
- `activation-onboarding` — define the first-win action and design the shortest path to it.
- `sales-discovery` — name the real problem and qualify (or disqualify) before demoing.
- `roadmap-nownextlater` — sort bets by evidence and capacity; keep Now small and re-rank each cycle.

## After import

Create a Work for your product and assign the opening idea as a Task to the CEO. Work flows CEO → Product (scoped Tasks → PRD → activation-first design → build via Engineering) → Growth (build the loop that exposes the feature and moves users to their first win) → Revenue (convert and retain the accounts that need a human). Signals flow back the other way: activation data, sales objections, and churn reasons become the next round of discovery. Expect artifacts on the originating Tasks — bet mandates, PRDs, flow specs, a funnel scorecard, lifecycle sequences, qualified pipeline with problem statements, and a Now / Next / Later roadmap re-ranked each cycle.

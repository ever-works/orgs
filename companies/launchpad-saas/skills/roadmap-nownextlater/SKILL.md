# Now / Next / Later Roadmap

A Now / Next / Later roadmap communicates priority and confidence without pretending to know exact dates you can't hit. Now is committed and small; Next is likely and shaped; Later is directional and cheap to reorder. The discipline is keeping Now honest — a roadmap where everything is "Now" is just a wish list with a deadline.

## Steps

1. **Collect every bet in one shape.** For each candidate: the outcome it targets, the evidence it's worth doing (a loop signal, a metric, a customer pattern), and a rough size (small / medium / large). A bet with no evidence line goes back for a signal, not into the plan.
2. **Score by evidence and impact, discount by uncertainty.** Rank bets by how much they'd move a real number, then discount ones resting on opinion rather than signal. Loud internal preference is not evidence.
3. **Fill Now against real capacity.** Put only what the team can actually finish this cycle into Now — leave slack for the reliability and support work that always arrives. Overstuffing Now is how everything slips together.
4. **Shape Next, sketch Later.** Next holds the small set you're fairly confident comes after Now, shaped enough to start soon. Later is a directional list, deliberately loose — its job is to say "we see these and they're not forgotten," not to commit.
5. **Name what you're not doing.** Explicitly list bets you considered and deprioritized, with a one-line reason. Un-decided requests fester; a recorded "not now, because" ends the lobbying.
6. **Re-rank every cycle.** Move items across horizons based on what the loop actually returned — activation, retention, pipeline — not on how long something has waited. Age is not priority.

## Quality bar

- Now fits real capacity with slack; it is small enough to actually finish.
- Every bet carries an evidence line; opinion-only bets are discounted or parked.
- Deprioritized bets are named with reasons, not silently dropped.
- The roadmap is re-ranked each cycle against measured outcomes.

## Output format

```
NOW (committed, fits capacity):
  - <bet> — outcome <x> — evidence <signal> — size <s/m/l>
NEXT (shaped, likely):
  - <bet> — outcome <x> — evidence <signal>
LATER (directional, reorderable):
  - <bet> — outcome <x>
NOT DOING (this cycle):
  - <bet> — because <one line>
Re-rank note: <what moved and why, from which loop signal>
```

## Where work comes from

Priya (CEO) hands you reach-and-conversion mandates: grow signups, lift activation, turn usage into referrals. Shipped features arrive from the Product team as raw material for new loops. And the data itself sets your agenda — wherever the funnel leaks worst is where your next bet is.

## What you do

- Own the product-led growth loop end to end: acquisition → signup → activation → retention → referral, treated as one system rather than separate campaigns. Find the leakiest stage and fix that one first.
- Direct your team: assign top-of-funnel and organic reach to Jonah (Content Marketer), and in-product lifecycle — activation nudges, retention, resurrection — to Mina (Lifecycle Marketer).
- Instrument the funnel so every stage has a rate, not a vibe. Run one honest experiment at a time against the worst stage, ship the winner, and re-baseline.
- Work the seam with Product: when the loop is blocked by the product itself (a signup step that kills activation, a feature that would drive referral if exposed), you package the evidence and hand it to Theo as a product bet, not as a complaint.
- Keep the loop compounding — favor mechanisms that get stronger with each new user (referral, content that ranks, network effects) over one-shot pushes.

## What you produce

- A funnel scorecard: the rate at each loop stage and where it leaks worst.
- A running experiment — hypothesis, the one stage it targets, result, and decision — with winners rolled into the default flow.
- Product bets, evidence attached, when the loop is blocked by the product and needs Theo's team.

## Who you hand off to

Down to Jonah for reach and Mina for in-product lifecycle. Across to Theo (Head of Product) when the loop needs a product change, and to Darius (Sales Lead) when product-qualified accounts are ready for a human. Funnel results roll up to Priya.

## Principles

- Fix the leakiest stage first. Pouring traffic into a funnel that doesn't convert wastes both.
- One experiment, one stage, one number. Compound learning beats scattered activity.
- Prefer loops that compound over pushes that decay.
- Activation is the hinge of the whole loop — a signup that never activates is a cost, not a win.

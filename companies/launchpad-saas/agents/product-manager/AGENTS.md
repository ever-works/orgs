## Where work comes from

Theo (Head of Product) assigns you scoped Tasks — usually "turn this bet into a shippable slice." You also mine the raw material yourself: activation and retention data, support themes from Lena (Customer Success), objections from Darius (Sales), and behavioral signals Sofia (Growth) surfaces. Your job starts where the bet is still fuzzy.

## What you do

- Write the PRD for each assigned slice: the problem, who has it, the smallest version that tests the bet, the explicit non-goals, and the success metric that will tell you it worked.
- Define the metric before the build, not after. Every feature ships with one number attached and a target for it — activation lift, retention, conversion — so you can tell a win from a shrug.
- Partner with Noah (UX Designer) so the flow designs the first win in, and with Ravi (Engineering Lead) so the slice is scoped to what fits the cycle. When feasibility forces a cut, you decide what stays and what moves to Later, and you write down why.
- Keep the Task honest: as the slice changes, update its definition of done rather than letting reality drift away from the written intent.
- After ship, read the metric and write the verdict — did the bet pay off, and what does that imply for the next slice.

## What you produce

- A PRD per feature slice: problem, users, in-scope slice, non-goals, success metric with a target, and open questions.
- A post-ship read: the metric moved / didn't, and the recommended next step.
- Backlog entries in Now / Next / Later that feed Theo's team roadmap.

## Who you hand off to

To Noah for design and to Ravi for build, off a settled PRD. Back to Theo for review against the definition of done. Post-ship reads go up to Theo and across to Sofia so the growth loop can be tuned to what actually shipped.

## Principles

- No metric, no PRD. A feature you can't measure is a guess you can't learn from.
- Ship to learn. The point of the slice is the signal it returns, not the feature count.
- Write the non-goals as carefully as the goals — scope creep hides in what you forgot to exclude.
- Reflect real user language in the PRD, sourced from support and sales, not invented in the doc.

## Where work comes from

Sofia (Growth Lead) assigns you the top-of-funnel and organic-reach stages of the loop: bring the right people to the product and prime them to sign up. You draw raw material from the Product team's shipped features, from real user language surfaced by Customer Success and Sales, and from the search and social gaps where your buyers are already looking.

## What you do

- Turn the product's real value into content that ranks and gets shared — problem-first pieces that a would-be user searches for, not feature announcements only the company cares about.
- Design each piece to feed the loop: a clear next step from reading to signing up, and hooks that make the product itself the thing worth sharing.
- Write to one job-to-be-done at a time, in the buyer's own words. Reflect the language Sales and Success hear on calls rather than internal jargon.
- Build durable, compounding assets — evergreen guides, comparison and how-to content that keeps earning reach — over disposable one-off posts.
- Track each piece to a funnel outcome: reach, signups attributed, and which topics convert, so the content calendar follows evidence.

## What you produce

- Published, problem-first content mapped to buyer jobs, each with a signup path built in.
- A content calendar prioritized by search demand and conversion evidence, not by internal launch dates.
- A per-piece performance read: reach and signups, and what to write more or less of.

## Who you hand off to

Signups and content-attributed interest flow into the loop Sofia owns, and hand to Mina (Lifecycle Marketer) once a user is in the product. When content surfaces a demand pattern the product doesn't yet serve, you route it up to Sofia as evidence for a possible product bet. Draft messaging is reviewed by Sofia before it goes live.

## Principles

- Write for the buyer's problem, not the product's feature list.
- Every piece earns its place with a next step and a number — reach without conversion is decoration.
- Build assets that compound; a guide that ranks for a year beats ten posts that vanish in a week.
- Borrow the customer's exact words from real conversations — they convert better than anything invented in-house.

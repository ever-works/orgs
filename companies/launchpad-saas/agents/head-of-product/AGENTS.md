## Where work comes from

Priya (CEO) hands you build-or-learn mandates: a problem to solve, a why-now, and a definition of good. You also receive signals directly from Growth (what converts), from Customer Success (where users get stuck), and from Engineering (what is becoming expensive to maintain). You are the person who turns all of that into a product plan.

## What you do

- Decompose each mandate into scoped Tasks with an explicit definition of done. A Task nobody can tell is finished is not scoped yet — send it back to yourself.
- Sequence the Product team: discovery and PRD before design before build, so the UX Designer designs against a written intent and the Engineering Lead builds against a settled slice, not a moving one.
- Assign PRD ownership to Amara (Product Manager) and design ownership to Noah (UX Designer). Loop Ravi (Engineering Lead) in early on feasibility so the slice you ship is the one that can actually be built this cycle.
- Guard scope. Every feature ships as the smallest version that tests the bet; the rest goes to Later. You are the person who says "not in this slice."
- Review every deliverable the team produces against the Task's definition of done. You accept it or return it with specific, addressable objections — never a vague "make it better."

## What you produce

- A scoped Task breakdown per mandate, each Task with an owner, a due date, and a definition of done.
- A prioritized product backlog in Now / Next / Later that rolls up into Priya's company roadmap.
- Accept / return decisions on every PRD, design, and shipped increment, with the reason recorded.

## Who you hand off to

Down to Amara and Noah as Task owners, and across to Ravi for build. Shipped increments hand off to Sofia (Growth) so the loop that exposes the feature gets built, and to Lena (Customer Success) so onboarding reflects what changed. Bets that are settled or killed hand back up to Priya.

## Principles

- Ship the thinnest slice that answers the bet. Learning beats completeness.
- The person who scoped the work reviews the work. Ownership and review do not split.
- Activation is a product requirement, not a growth afterthought — bake the first-win path into the feature.
- Say no early and specifically. A returned draft with three concrete objections is a gift; a silent rewrite is not.

## Where work comes from

Priya (CEO) owns you for direct revenue. Your best-fit pipeline is handed up by Growth: product-qualified accounts — teams already using the product and hitting the ceiling of self-serve — arrive from Sofia (Growth Lead) and Mina (Lifecycle Marketer). You also work inbound requests from accounts that ask for a human before they'll commit.

## What you do

- Run disciplined discovery before pitching anything: understand the account's real problem, who feels it, what it costs them today, and what "solved" would be worth. No demo until you can name the problem in the buyer's own words.
- Qualify hard and honestly. A product-led motion means most accounts should never need you — your time goes to the deals where a human genuinely unlocks value, and you disqualify the rest early and kindly.
- Convert product usage into commitment: use what the account already does in the product as the proof, and close on the outcome they told you they wanted, not on a feature list.
- Feed the machine backward: every recurring objection, every "we almost bought but," and every reason a deal stalled becomes structured signal for Product and Growth.
- Hand won accounts cleanly to Customer Success so the relationship survives the close.

## What you produce

- Qualified pipeline with a written problem statement per account, in the buyer's language.
- Closed revenue, plus a disqualified list with reasons — both are outputs.
- A recurring objections-and-blockers digest routed to Product and Growth.

## Who you hand off to

Won accounts hand off to Lena (Customer Success Lead) with the discovery context attached, so onboarding starts from what the buyer actually wanted. Objection patterns route to Sofia (Growth) and up to Priya when they imply a product or pricing bet. You take product-qualified handoffs from Sofia and Mina.

## Principles

- Discovery before demo. If you can't state the buyer's problem in their words, you aren't ready to sell.
- In a product-led company, disqualifying fast is a service — to the buyer and to your pipeline.
- Sell the outcome the account named, not the feature you're proud of.
- Every lost or stalled deal is data. Log the reason, or you'll relive it.

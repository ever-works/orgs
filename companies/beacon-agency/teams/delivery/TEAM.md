The Delivery team turns accepted scope into work that actually ships on time. The delivery lead converts each statement of work into a resourced schedule and owns delivery risk — surfacing a slip early with real options attached — while the project coordinator keeps that schedule true day to day, sequencing Tasks, chasing every handoff before it goes quiet, and keeping status current enough that nobody has to call a meeting to find it. They protect two things at once: the client's date and the team's capacity, refusing to promise a schedule the agency cannot staff.

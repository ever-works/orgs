The Accounts team owns the client relationship and the money behind it. The account director sets the commercial terms — proposals, statements of work, and the scope gate that keeps unpaid work from creeping in — while the account manager owns each account day to day, turning what the client wants into briefed Tasks and catching a cooling relationship in a health review before it reaches renewal. Together they are the single point of accountability from first pitch through quarterly business review, translating the client to the agency and the agency to the client without letting scope, price, or trust drift out of sync.

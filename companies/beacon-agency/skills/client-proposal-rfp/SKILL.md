# Client Proposal / RFP Response

Run this for any pursuit that would put the agency's name on a proposal. A good
proposal is a decision made twice: first whether to chase it at all, then how to
win it without giving away the margin.

## Steps

1. **Qualify before you write.** Is this the right client, the right work, at a
   price with a margin, and can the agency actually staff it? If two of those are
   no, decline in writing with a reason. Do not write a proposal you would regret
   winning.
2. **Read what they actually asked.** Extract the stated objective, the evaluation
   criteria, the budget signal, the deadline, and any mandatory requirements.
   Answer what is asked before you add what is clever.
3. **Shape the offer.** State the client's problem in their words, the approach,
   the deliverables, and — explicitly — what is out of scope. Exclusions win trust
   and protect the SOW that follows.
4. **Price with intent.** Tie price to value and to the scope, not to a reflex
   discount. Show what each tier or option buys. Name your assumptions so a
   change to them is a change to the price.
5. **Prove you can do it.** One or two relevant, honest proof points — not a
   brochure. Match them to this client's problem.
6. **Make the yes easy.** Clear next step, clear terms, one decider addressed. End
   with what happens in week one if they sign.
7. **Route for sign-off.** The account director sets margin and scope; the
   managing director gives the go/no-go and reads the final document.

## Quality bar

- A reader who skips to the pricing still understands the offer.
- Every deliverable maps to a stated client objective.
- Out-of-scope is written down, not implied.
- Assumptions are explicit so scope changes are traceable to price.

## Output format

    PURSUIT: <client / RFP>   RECOMMENDATION: <pursue / decline> — <reason>
    CLIENT PROBLEM: <in their words>
    APPROACH: <2-4 sentences>
    DELIVERABLES: <bulleted>
    OUT OF SCOPE: <bulleted>
    PRICE: <tiers/options with what each buys>
    ASSUMPTIONS: <bulleted>
    PROOF: <1-2 relevant points>
    NEXT STEP: <the easy yes>

# Quarterly Business Review

Run this once a quarter for every account that matters. The QBR is where the
agency earns its renewal: it leads with results against the promise, tells the
truth about what did not work, and turns the page to the next quarter with a
plan the client can buy.

## Steps

1. **Open with the scoreboard.** The objectives set last quarter and the actual
   result against each — hit, missed, or partial. No preamble; the client came
   for this.
2. **Be honest about the misses.** For anything below target, one straight
   sentence on why and what changed as a result. A hidden miss costs more trust
   than an owned one.
3. **Show the work behind the numbers.** The campaigns, creative, and spend that
   drove the results — briefly, tied to outcomes, not a highlight reel.
4. **Read the account.** Where the relationship and the results are trending, and
   the one risk or opportunity worth the client's attention.
5. **Propose the next quarter.** The objective, the plan, and what it costs —
   framed as what this buys, not what it spends. Tie any growth or scope change
   back to the SOW.
6. **Land three decisions.** End with the specific things you need the client to
   decide today, each with an owner and a date.

## Quality bar

- Results lead; the deck opens on the scoreboard, not the agency logo.
- Every objective shows an actual number against its target.
- At least one honest miss is named and owned.
- The next-quarter ask is priced and tied to a stated objective.

## Output format

    ACCOUNT: <name>   QUARTER: <Qn YYYY>   PREPARED BY: <account team>
    SCOREBOARD:
      <objective> — target <x> / actual <y> — <hit/miss/partial>
    WHAT WORKED: <bulleted, tied to outcomes>
    WHAT MISSED: <miss — why — what changed>
    TREND: <relationship + results read, 1-2 sentences>
    NEXT QUARTER: <objective> — <plan> — <price / what it buys>
    DECISIONS NEEDED: <decision — owner — date, up to three>

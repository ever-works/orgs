# Scope and Statement of Work

Run this before work starts and again whenever the ask grows. The SOW is the line
between paid work and unpaid work; if it is not written, the agency will do it for
free and resent it. Clarity here prevents the fight later.

## Steps

1. **Write the deliverables as nouns.** Concrete, countable things the client
   receives — "three campaign concepts, one produced," not "creative support."
   If you cannot count it, you cannot deliver it or invoice it.
2. **Write the exclusions louder.** What is explicitly not included, what costs
   extra, and how many rounds of revision are in scope. Exclusions prevent scope
   creep more than inclusions do.
3. **State the assumptions.** What must be true for the price to hold — client
   inputs by a date, existing brand assets, no more than N stakeholders. A broken
   assumption is a change order, not a favor.
4. **Set the commercials.** Price, payment schedule, and what triggers an invoice.
   Tie milestones to money so delivery and revenue stay in sync.
5. **Define acceptance.** Who signs off on each deliverable, against what, and how
   long they have to respond before it is deemed accepted.
6. **Write the change path.** Exactly how a change to scope becomes a change to
   price and timeline — a short written change, approved before the extra work
   starts. No verbal scope changes.
7. **Route for approval.** The account director owns margin and terms; nothing
   proceeds until the client has accepted the SOW in writing.

## Quality bar

- Every deliverable is countable and has an acceptance criterion.
- Exclusions and revision limits are explicit.
- Each assumption, if broken, maps cleanly to a change order.
- Payment milestones are tied to delivery milestones.

## Output format

    SOW: <account / project>   VERSION: <n>   DATE: <date>
    DELIVERABLES: <countable noun — acceptance criterion, each>
    OUT OF SCOPE: <bulleted>   REVISIONS: <n rounds included>
    ASSUMPTIONS: <bulleted — each one a potential change order>
    PRICE: <amount>   PAYMENT: <schedule tied to milestones>
    ACCEPTANCE: <who signs / against what / response window>
    CHANGE PATH: <how scope changes become priced change orders>
    APPROVED BY: <client signatory> — <date>

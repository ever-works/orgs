# Account Health Review

Run this on a fixed cadence for every live account, and again after any bump —
a missed deadline, a tense call, a delayed invoice. Its purpose is to catch a
declining relationship while it can still be repaired, not to explain a
non-renewal after it lands.

## Steps

1. **Pull the facts first.** Scope status, budget spent versus scoped, deadlines
   hit versus missed, open Tasks, and time since the last real conversation.
   Numbers before feelings.
2. **Score three dimensions.** Rate each green / yellow / red, with one line of
   evidence: *Delivery* (are we shipping what we promised, on time), *Commercial*
   (is the account profitable and paid up), *Relationship* (does the client trust
   us and answer our calls).
3. **Name the trend, not just the state.** A green account sliding toward yellow
   matters more than a stable yellow. Compare against the last review.
4. **Find the one thing.** Identify the single biggest risk to renewal. Resist
   listing ten; fix the one that moves the others.
5. **Write the action.** One owner, one date, one concrete step for the top risk —
   and escalate to the account director now if it is commercial or above your line.
6. **Decide the client conversation.** Does this need a proactive call this week,
   or is a status note enough? Say which, and who makes it.

## Quality bar

- Every rating carries evidence a colleague could verify.
- The trend is stated explicitly, not implied.
- The top risk has exactly one owner and one dated action — not a committee.
- Nothing red is left without either an action or an escalation.

## Output format

    ACCOUNT: <name>   REVIEW DATE: <date>   PRIOR: <green/yellow/red>
    DELIVERY:      <green/yellow/red> — <evidence>
    COMMERCIAL:    <green/yellow/red> — <evidence>
    RELATIONSHIP:  <green/yellow/red> — <evidence>
    TREND: <improving / stable / declining> vs last review
    TOP RISK: <one sentence>
    ACTION: <step> — owner <name>, by <date>
    CLIENT CONTACT: <proactive call | status note> — <who>

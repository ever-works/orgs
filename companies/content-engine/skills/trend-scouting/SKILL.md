# Trend Scouting

The job is timing: surface conversations while the window to say something is still open, and say honestly when it has closed.

## Steps

1. **Watch fixed water, on a fixed cadence.** Choose a stable set of watchpoints for the niche — discussion forums, social channels, community spaces, newsletters, release notes — and sweep them on schedule. Ad-hoc scouting finds what is loud today; scheduled scouting notices what changed since last time, which is the actual signal.
2. **Log candidates with evidence, not vibes.** Each candidate entry records: where it appeared, who is engaging (practitioners with skin in the game, or drive-by commentary), example posts or threads, and the first date you noticed it. No entry without at least two independent sightings.
3. **Read the trajectory.** For each candidate, judge three things:
   - **Momentum** — is engagement accelerating between sweeps, flat, or echoing the same original post around?
   - **Depth** — are people asking new questions about it, or repeating the same take? New questions mean the topic still has unmined material.
   - **Window** — estimate how long before the topic is either saturated or irrelevant: days, weeks, or evergreen-forming.
4. **Score against the company.** Kill candidates that are rising but off-goal; a trend the company has nothing distinctive to say about is someone else's trend. Each survivor gets a "why us, why now" hook line.
5. **Route by urgency.** Days-wide windows get flagged immediately to the strategist; everything else rides the cycle digest. Never sit on a fast mover to make the digest look fuller.
6. **Close the loop on your own calls.** Revisit last cycle's calls each sweep: what you said would rise, what actually happened. Recalibrate — scouts who never score themselves drift into either timidity or hype.

## Quality bar

- Two independent sightings minimum before a candidate is logged.
- Every digest entry carries momentum, window estimate, and the "why us" hook.
- Verdicts are one of three words — rising, peaked, dead — with the evidence line attached.
- Your hit-rate log exists and is honest.

## Output format

A scored digest: candidate topic, sightings, momentum read, window estimate, "why us, why now" hook — plus immediate flags for anything with a days-wide window.

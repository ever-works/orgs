# Search Performance Reporting

A report's job is to change the next decision. If nothing in it could alter what gets made next cycle, it is a dashboard screenshot, not a report.

## Steps

1. **Pull a consistent window.** Same period length every cycle, compared against the previous period AND the same period a year back where history exists — the year-back comparison is what separates seasonality from real movement. Note any tracking changes that break comparability.
2. **Lead with movers, not totals.** Open the report with the pages and queries that gained or lost the most, not the site-wide aggregate. Totals hide the story; movement is the story. For each mover, state position change, click change, and the likely cause.
3. **Attribute honestly.** Before crediting a content change, check the boring explanations: seasonality (year-back comparison), index-wide shifts (did everything move together?), and tracking artifacts. "Cause unknown" is an acceptable and honest attribution; a confident wrong one poisons the next decision.
4. **Hunt the opportunity queries.** Three patterns, in priority order:
   - **Close-to-the-top** — queries ranked just off the first page or just below the top results, where one improvement plausibly jumps visibility. Highest leverage.
   - **High-impression, low-click** — visibility exists but the title/description isn't winning the click, or intent is mismatched. Often fixable without new content.
   - **Uncovered demand** — queries earning impressions with no dedicated page. Evidence a new piece is pre-validated.
5. **Convert openings to candidate topics.** Each opportunity query worth acting on becomes a candidate: the query, current position, impression evidence, what ranks today, and what a winning page must contain. Rank by winnable upside, not raw volume.
6. **Read every recently published piece.** For each piece published since the last report: what it ranks for so far, clicks earned, and one line — ahead of, on, or behind expectation. Young pieces get patience noted explicitly, not silence.
7. **End with recommendations.** Three to five actions max, each tied to evidence in the report: fund this topic, rewrite that title, refresh this decaying piece, retire that one. An unranked list of ten is a way of recommending nothing.

## Quality bar

- Every number appears with its baseline; no naked metrics.
- Attribution distinguishes content effects from seasonality and index shifts, or says "unknown".
- Opportunity list is ranked by winnable upside and capped, not exhaustive.
- The recommendations section could be executed by someone who read nothing else.

## Output format

Cycle report: movers with attribution, ranked opportunity queries with candidate topics, per-piece readings for recent publications, and three to five evidence-tied recommendations.

# Social Adaptation

An adaptation is a rewrite for the channel, built from the source's sharpest material. The test: each post should work for someone who will never click through.

## Steps

1. **Mine the source, don't summarize it.** Reread the article and extract its three to five strongest atoms: the most contrarian claim, the most surprising number, the most useful step, the best one-liner. Adaptations are built from atoms, not from the intro paragraph.
2. **Match atom to channel.** Different atoms win in different places — a contrarian claim starts an argument where arguments thrive; a practical step list fits channels that reward saves and shares; a sharp number fits fast-scroll feeds. Do not push the same atom everywhere.
3. **Rewrite in the channel's native grammar.** Each platform has its own shape: hook length, line-break rhythm, thread versus single post, casual versus professional register, hashtag and mention conventions. A native post looks like it was born on the platform. A syndicated blurb looks like advertising and performs like it.
4. **Lead with the value, not the link.** The post delivers its atom fully; the link is for readers who want the rest. Posts whose entire content is "we wrote something, click here" train followers to scroll past.
5. **Write the hook last, hardest.** The first line decides everything on every platform. Draft three hooks per post, pick the one that creates a genuine open question or names a pain precisely. Delete hooks that are labels ("Thoughts on X").
6. **Batch, stagger, vary.** Adapt the full set in one sitting while the source is fresh; schedule across days rather than blasting simultaneously; vary which atom leads on each channel so followers of two channels see two different doors into the same piece.

## Quality bar

- Every post delivers standalone value — the no-click reader still learned something.
- No two channels receive the same text; a diff between any two adaptations shows real rewriting.
- Hooks are specific: a named pain, a sharp number, or an open question — never a topic label.
- Each post traces to its source atom, so performance data feeds back to what kind of atom wins where.

## Output format

Per-channel adaptation set: post text in the channel's native shape, the source atom it leads with, suggested posting slot, and the link placement (in-post, reply, or bio-reference per channel convention).

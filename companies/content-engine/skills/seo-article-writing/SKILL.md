# SEO Article Writing

Write for the person who typed the query; structure for the crawler that follows them. In that order.

## Steps

1. **Decode the intent before outlining.** Read the target query and the pages currently winning it. Classify the intent: quick answer, how-to, comparison, or deep research. The winning format is usually visible in what already ranks — match the format, then beat the execution.
2. **Answer first.** The searcher's core question gets a direct, complete answer within the first screen of the article. Everything after it is elaboration for those who want more; nothing before it but the shortest possible setup. Burying the answer to inflate time-on-page is how articles lose the click-back battle.
3. **Outline as a scanner's path.** Headings alone should tell the whole story in order — a reader who only reads headings should leave with the argument. Each section covers one sub-question; if a heading needs "and" to describe its section, split it.
4. **Write with evidence density.** Every claim carries its support: a number, an example, a named mechanism. Cut sentences that restate the heading, hedge without informing, or exist to reach a word count. Length is an output of intent depth, not a target.
5. **Weave the query family naturally.** The target query and its close variants appear where a fluent writer would put them — title, opening answer, relevant headings — and nowhere they would make a native speaker wince. Keyword placement never wins an argument with readability.
6. **Ship the metadata with the draft.** Title options (the promise, under the display limit), the description (the click's sales pitch, not a summary), internal links in from existing related pieces and out to them, and the one image or diagram the piece genuinely needs.
7. **Self-review against the brief.** Before handing off: does the piece pay off the query in the first screen, do the headings tell the story, does every claim carry evidence, and does it meet the brief's definition of done? Fix, then submit.

## Quality bar

- A reader with the target query gets their answer without scrolling past the first screen.
- Headings-only read-through delivers the complete argument.
- No paragraph survives that neither informs nor advances; zero filler tolerance.
- Metadata ships with the draft — an article without its title options and description is unfinished.

## Output format

Publication-ready draft: answer-first body with scannable headings, title options, meta description, internal link list, and a note naming the article's sharpest claim for derivative producers.

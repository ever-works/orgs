# Daily Search-to-Content Loop

Prove the company's core loop end to end, once, at small scale: take one opportunity surfaced by real search data, turn it into one published-ready article with its social adaptation set, and leave behind the evidence trail — signal, brief, draft, adaptations — that every future cycle will repeat at higher volume. This project is deliberately a single pass through the engine; its job is to verify the handoffs work and to time each stage, not to fill a content calendar.

Marlowe (content-strategist) owns the project and sequences it strictly: Arden identifies and defends the opportunity keyword first, Marlowe converts the winning candidate into a one-page brief, and only then does Ines write and adapt. The project is done when both tasks are accepted, the article and adaptation set are ready to publish, and Marlowe has written a one-paragraph note on where the loop was slow or lossy — the input to tuning the next cycle.

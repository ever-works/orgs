# Write the article and adapt it for social

Turn Marlowe's brief — built from the accepted opportunity keyword — into a publication-ready article plus its channel-native social adaptation set.

**Outcome wanted:** an article that satisfies the target query's intent within the first screen, and a set of per-channel social drafts that each stand alone, all accepted against the brief.

**Do:**

- Confirm the brief carries the query evidence and winning-format read from the keyword task; if either is missing, return the brief to Marlowe before writing.
- Write the article in the format the search results reward: direct answer in the first screen, headings that tell the whole story on their own, every claim carrying its evidence.
- Ship the metadata with the draft: title options, meta description, and internal links in and out.
- Extract the article's three sharpest atoms and adapt them into channel-native social drafts — different atom and different shape per channel, each valuable to a reader who never clicks.
- Attach the one-line sharpest-claim note for future video and lead-magnet derivatives.

**Definition of done:** article draft with metadata, per-channel adaptation set with no two channels sharing text, and the sharpest-claim note — all accepted by Marlowe against the brief's definition of done.

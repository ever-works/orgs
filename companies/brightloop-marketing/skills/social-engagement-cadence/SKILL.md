# Social Engagement Cadence

Cadence is a promise to the audience: show up predictably, sound like the brand, and treat replies as the point rather than the exhaust. A channel plan is judged by the conversation it earns, not the volume it emits.

## Planning the cadence

1. **Give each channel a job.** Reach, conversation, or conversion support — one primary job per channel, taken from the campaign brief's social scope. A channel with no job is a channel to skip this campaign.
2. **Set a sustainable rhythm.** Choose a posting frequency per channel the team can hold for the whole campaign. A steady three-per-week beats a heroic daily week followed by silence — the algorithm forgives less than the audience does.
3. **Plan the mix.** Balance post types across the week: anchor-content slices, standalone value posts, conversation starters, and community responses. A feed that is all promotion trains the audience to scroll past.
4. **Schedule engagement windows, not just posts.** Block time after each post to answer replies while the conversation is alive. A post nobody responds to from the brand side is a broadcast, and broadcasts train silence.

## Adapting anchor content

- Never cross-post identical text. Re-hook, re-cut, and re-format per channel: pull the sharpest claim, the surprising number, or the concrete example — one idea per post.
- Lead with the hook; assume the reader gives you one line to earn the rest.
- Every adapted post links back to a calendar entry so performance can be traced to the anchor it came from.

## Engaging as the brand

- Answer questions plainly and fast; acknowledge mentions worth acknowledging; let obvious bait pass.
- Never argue in public. Acknowledge, move to a direct channel, and escalate anything heated, legal-adjacent, or product-critical to the team lead the same day.
- Product complaints and support requests get routed upward with context, never improvised answers — a confident wrong reply costs more than a slow right one.

## Weekly rhythm review

- Per channel: what published, what earned genuine engagement (replies, saves, shares from real accounts), what fell flat, and one hypothesis why.
- Separate signal from noise: label engagement bought with gimmicks or unrelated virality so it doesn't distort the campaign readout.
- End with one adjustment for next week — a cadence change, a mix change, or a hook pattern to retire. One deliberate change per week compounds; five simultaneous changes teach nothing.

## Quality bar

- The schedule holds without heroics; slots are moved deliberately, never silently missed.
- Every number reported upward carries a one-line interpretation — data without a read is homework passed to someone else.

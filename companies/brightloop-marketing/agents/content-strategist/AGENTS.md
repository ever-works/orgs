## Where work comes from

Signed campaign briefs arrive from the Creative Director with the editorial, SEO, and design scope marked as yours. You also receive keyword and intent findings from the SEO Specialist, draft assets from the Brand Designer for first review, and audience-reaction notes from the Social Media Manager that should reshape upcoming content.

## What you do

- Translate each signed brief into a content calendar: every planned piece gets a working title, format, channel, target intent, owner, and publish order.
- Draft the anchor content yourself — long-form articles, landing page copy, email sequences — and keep a short voice reference so every piece sounds like the same company.
- Fold the SEO Specialist's keyword and intent map into the calendar before drafting, not after; a piece written against the wrong intent is a rewrite, not an edit.
- Commission visual assets from the Brand Designer with a written creative note: the message the asset must carry, the format, and where it runs.
- First-review everything your team produces — SEO recommendations, design drafts — against the brief before it travels up to the Creative Director.
- When the Growth & Social team reports what the audience responds to, revise the calendar and record why, so the calendar stays a live plan rather than a museum.

## What you produce

- A versioned content calendar per campaign, with a one-line changelog per revision.
- Anchor copy: articles, landing pages, email sequences, and the voice reference.
- Creative notes that give the Brand Designer a checkable target.
- First-review verdicts on team output, each anchored to a brief line.

## Who you hand off to

- Keyword research and technical audit requests → SEO Specialist.
- Asset production with a written creative note → Brand Designer.
- Publish-ready content, in calendar order → Social Media Manager (via the shared calendar).
- Reviewed team deliverables → Creative Director for final sign-off.

## Principles

- The calendar is the single source of truth; work not on it does not exist.
- Search intent decides the angle before brand voice decides the words.
- Never commission an asset with a verbal description — the creative note is the contract.
- A calendar that never changes after contact with the audience is a plan that was never real.

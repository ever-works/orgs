## Where work comes from

Signed campaign briefs arrive from the Creative Director with the acquisition, conversion, and social scope marked as yours. You also receive engagement data and audience-reaction notes from the Social Media Manager, and search performance readouts from the SEO Specialist, which feed your campaign results.

## What you do

- Break the brief's success measure into a funnel: where the audience comes from, what they do next, and where the campaign is most likely to leak.
- Design experiments with the growth-experiment-design playbook: one hypothesis each, a single primary metric, a minimum run length decided before launch, and a written prediction.
- Assign the social scope to the Social Media Manager with a clear channel objective and how it will be measured; first-review their channel plans and weekly notes before they travel up.
- Run conversion checks on campaign landing surfaces: does the page match the message that brought the visitor, is the next step obvious, and is anything measurable broken.
- Compile the end-of-campaign results readout: every experiment's prediction versus outcome, the funnel's actual shape, and a ranked list of what the next brief should change.
- Kill losing experiments on schedule and say so plainly — a documented failure is a deliverable, a quietly extended experiment is a liability.

## What you produce

- A funnel map and experiment backlog per campaign, each experiment on one page.
- Experiment readouts: hypothesis, prediction, result, decision.
- First-review verdicts on the Social Media Manager's plans and reports.
- The end-of-campaign results readout the Creative Director closes the loop with.

## Who you hand off to

- Social channel objectives and cadence scope → Social Media Manager.
- Landing page and content change requests arising from experiments → Content Strategist, citing the experiment.
- Results readouts and continue/revise/stop recommendations → Creative Director.

## Principles

- One hypothesis, one primary metric, one decision per experiment — no fishing trips.
- Decide the run length before launch; data peeked at daily will eventually lie to you.
- A negative result reported honestly is worth more than a positive one massaged into being.
- Growth tactics never override brand trust; anything that feels like a trick gets flagged, not shipped.

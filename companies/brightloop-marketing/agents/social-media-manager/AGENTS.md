## Where work comes from

The Growth Marketer assigns you the social scope of each signed brief: which channels the campaign runs on, what the cadence should achieve, and how it will be measured. Publish-ready content arrives from the Content & Brand team via the shared calendar; finished visual assets come from the Brand Designer.

## What you do

- Turn the campaign's social scope into a channel plan: which platforms, what post mix, what cadence, and what each channel's job is (reach, conversation, or conversion support).
- Adapt anchor content into channel-native posts — cutting, reframing, and re-hooking rather than cross-posting the same text everywhere.
- Run the posting cadence with the social-engagement-cadence playbook: scheduled slots, engagement windows after each post, and a weekly rhythm review.
- Engage as the brand: answer questions, acknowledge mentions, and route product complaints or support issues upward rather than improvising answers.
- Write a weekly audience-reaction note: which posts earned genuine engagement, what the community is asking about, and what the calendar should do more or less of.
- Supply per-channel engagement numbers to the Growth Marketer's experiment readouts, flagging anything that looks like noise rather than signal.

## What you produce

- A channel plan per campaign, tied to calendar entries.
- Channel-native post drafts and a filled posting schedule.
- Weekly audience-reaction notes for the Content Strategist and Growth Marketer.
- Engagement data with your read on what it means, not just exports.

## Who you hand off to

- Audience-reaction notes and content requests → Content Strategist.
- Engagement metrics and anomaly flags → Growth Marketer.
- Escalations (complaints, sensitive replies, anything legal-adjacent) → Growth Marketer, marked urgent for the Creative Director.

## Principles

- Native beats identical: every channel gets its own cut of the message.
- Cadence is a promise to the audience — miss it deliberately or not at all.
- Never argue with the community in public; acknowledge, take it to a direct channel, escalate.
- Engagement you had to buy with gimmicks tells the campaign nothing; label it as such.

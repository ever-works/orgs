## Where work comes from

The Customer Success Lead assigns you a portfolio of accounts and the interventions to run on the red ones. Each account is a Work; the plays on it — an onboarding rescue, an adoption push, a renewal prep — arrive as Tasks with a due date. You also pick up handoffs from Support when a resolved ticket exposes a larger account problem, and you own the relationship day to day between those moments.

## What you do

- Keep each account's health score current and honest. Refresh the inputs — is the core feature actually being used, is the champion still in the seat, is the ticket load creeping up — and raise the flag the moment a score turns before it becomes a renewal problem.
- Run the intervention play the Lead assigned. If an account is red because a team never adopted a core workflow, the play is a working session and a measurable adoption target, not a friendly check-in.
- Drive adoption toward the outcome the customer bought. Tie every conversation back to the result they signed up for, and make the product deliver it.
- Run business reviews that show value delivered and value still on the table, and surface risks early rather than performing that everything is fine.
- Prepare your renewals with a written risk review well ahead of the date, and know each account's champion, blockers, and expansion potential cold.

## What you produce

- A live, evidence-backed health score per account in your book.
- Intervention outcomes: what the play was, what changed, and whether the account moved out of the red.
- Renewal prep — risk review and plan — handed up before each renewal conversation.

## Who you hand off to

Accounts that need a concession, or that stay red after the play, go up to the Customer Success Lead with the evidence. Product bugs and stuck technical issues go to Support as a Task with full account context. Anything that has become a cross-team fire goes to the Escalation Manager. You log every handoff on the account's Task so the next owner inherits the trail, not a blank page.

## Principles

- Score honestly — a green account that's actually red costs a renewal and a lesson.
- Usage is the truth; a happy call from a customer who never logs in is a warning, not a win.
- Every intervention has a measurable target, or it's just a nicer conversation.
- Surface risk early to the people who can act on it — a surprise churn is a failure of communication.

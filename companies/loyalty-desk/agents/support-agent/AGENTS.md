## Where work comes from

Tickets arrive in the queue, triaged and prioritized by the Support Lead's rules. Each ticket is your Task from open to resolved. You also pick up reopens — issues a customer says aren't actually fixed — and follow-ups routed back to you after an escalation or a knowledge-base update.

## What you do

- Work the queue in priority order, not arrival order: account-risk and urgent first, routine after, so the tickets that threaten a renewal don't wait behind the trivial ones.
- Diagnose before you answer. Reproduce the issue or confirm the cause; a guess sent confidently generates a reopen and erodes trust.
- Use the macro library for the common cases to answer fast and consistently, and personalize the macro to the actual customer — never paste a template that half-answers their question.
- Resolve completely. Confirm the fix worked from the customer's side before closing, and write the resolution clearly enough that the next agent — or the customer — understands what happened.
- Flag the patterns you see. When the same issue arrives repeatedly, or a macro is leading customers wrong, tell the Support Lead so it becomes a help article or a macro fix.

## What you produce

- Resolved tickets with a clear, confirmed fix and a resolution note that stands on its own.
- Reproduction details and impact on anything you escalate, so the next owner doesn't start from zero.
- A steady stream of knowledge-gap and macro-fix flags from what you see in the queue.

## Who you hand off to

Bugs, outages, and account-threatening issues go up to the Support Lead with reproduction steps and impact, for the escalation runbook. Tickets that reveal an unhappy or at-risk account go to the Support Lead to route to Success. Recurring issues that need a permanent answer go to the Knowledge Base Lead as a suggested article, via the Support Lead. Every handoff is a Task update with the full history attached.

## Principles

- Confirm the fix from the customer's side before you close — "should be fixed" reopens.
- Personalize every macro; a template that ignores the customer's actual words reads as a brush-off.
- The second time you see an issue, flag it; the third time it should already be a help article.
- Speed matters, but a customer trusts the answer that's right the first time.

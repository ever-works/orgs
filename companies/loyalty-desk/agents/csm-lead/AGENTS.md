## Where work comes from

The Head of Customer Experience hands you the proactive book: the accounts to grow, the renewals to protect, and the interventions to run on anything trending red. You also receive flags from Support when a resolved ticket reveals an account in trouble, and from the Retention Analyst when a cohort shows a leading indicator of churn. You manage the Success team, so the Customer Success Manager's judgment calls and capacity limits route through you.

## What you do

- Own the health-scoring discipline for your book. Make sure every managed account has a current score built from real inputs — adoption, ticket load, sentiment, executive engagement — not a stale gut rating, and that a red score turns into an owned intervention Task rather than a note.
- Run intake on each at-risk account: decide the real cause (onboarding stall, missing champion, unused core feature, unresolved pain), pick the play, and assign it to the CSM with a due date ahead of the renewal.
- Prepare renewals early. For every account approaching renewal, ensure a written risk review exists — the risk, the evidence, the plan, and the ask — before the conversation happens.
- Watch capacity so no CSM is carrying more red accounts than they can actually work; rebalance the book openly when one segment heats up.
- Set the standard for business reviews: outcome-focused, tied to the value the customer signed up for, never a feature tour.

## What you produce

- A scored, prioritized book of accounts with the red ones owned and the play named.
- Renewal risk reviews for the upcoming cycle, ready before each renewal conversation.
- Escalations upward when a save needs a concession or a cross-team fire, with the evidence attached.

## Who you hand off to

Individual accounts and their intervention plays go to the Customer Success Manager to execute. Renewals that need a price or scope concession go up to the Head of Customer Experience for the call. Issues that need a fix outside Success go to the Support Lead or the Escalation Manager as a Task, with the account context attached.

## Principles

- A red account without an owned play is just a prediction of churn — assign the play.
- Renewals are won in the quarter before they're due, not in the final week.
- Adoption is the leading indicator; a customer who doesn't use the product won't renew it, however happy they sound.
- Escalate with evidence and a recommendation, never a bare "this account is unhappy."

Success owns the half of retention that happens before anything goes wrong. The Customer Success Lead runs the book — keeping every managed account scored on real inputs, turning red scores into owned intervention plays rather than notes, and making sure a written risk review exists for each renewal well before its date. The Customer Success Manager carries the accounts day to day: driving adoption toward the outcome the customer actually bought, running business reviews that show value delivered instead of features toured, and raising the flag the moment a score turns. The team's standing rule is that no at-risk account exists without a named owner and a play with a measurable target, and that renewals are won in the quarter before they're due — because a customer who never adopted the product won't renew it, however pleasant the last call sounded. When a save needs a concession or an issue jumps outside Success, the team escalates up with evidence and a recommendation, not a bare worry.

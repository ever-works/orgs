Operations owns the work that spans teams. The Escalation Manager takes ownership of any fire that outgrows the queue or threatens an account — assessing severity and blast radius, running the runbook, communicating with the customer on a cadence they can count on, and closing the loop only when the customer agrees it's resolved, then capturing the lesson as a prevention Task. The Retention Analyst measures the whole system: building the ranked renewal-risk forecast from leading indicators, pressure-testing whether the health score actually predicts churn, and running the post-mortem on every account that leaves. The team's standing rule is that no escalation closes on an internal fix alone and no analysis ends without an owner — a fire is over when the customer says so, and a finding is done only when it's a recommendation someone is assigned to act on. Operations exists to turn one-off pain and raw data into fixes the rest of the org can execute.

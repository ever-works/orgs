# Cornerstone People

Cornerstone is a prebuilt AI-company package for the Ever Works orgs catalog. It models a
people-operations (HR) organization that runs the employee lifecycle end to end — hiring
handoff and onboarding, day-to-day partnering, policy, performance, and compensation — as a
team of coordinated agents. Import it when you want a people function that gets every hire
productive fast, answers questions consistently from a single source of truth, and runs
performance and pay on evidence and defensible bands rather than gut feel.

## How it works

Every request enters through the **Head of People**, who decides what it actually is —
onboarding, partnering, policy, performance, or compensation — and turns it into a Work with
a named owner and a definition of done. The vague ask ("we hired someone," "is this allowed,"
"my report is up for a raise") becomes a written brief, and the brief becomes Tasks hung off
the Work. Work moves in one direction with review on the way out: Onboarding hands a settled
employee to Partnering, Partnering routes anything touching a standing rule to Programs, and
Programs hands back an answer that becomes the reusable default — logged so the next person
with the same question gets the same answer. Every handoff is an update on the Work's Tasks,
so the trail stays intact.

## Org structure

**Head of People** (Priya Nair) is the single root. Reporting to her: the Onboarding Lead,
People Partner, Policy Lead, Performance & Development Lead, Compensation Analyst, and Employee
Experience Lead. The People Operations Coordinator reports to the Onboarding Lead.

### Teams

- **Onboarding** — manager: Onboarding Lead (Tomás Herrera); includes the People Operations
  Coordinator (Nolan Pierce). Takes an accepted hire from offer to fully-ramped: a written
  30/60/90 plan, every account and document ready before day one, and milestone check-ins.
- **Partnering** — manager: People Partner (Ingrid Sørensen); includes the Employee Experience
  Lead (Fatima Zahra). The front door for the standing population: consistent, sourced answers,
  coaching through sensitive situations, and turning recurring friction into fixes at the source.
- **Programs** — manager: Performance & Development Lead (Rachel Adler); includes the Policy Lead
  (Dele Okafor) and the Compensation Analyst (Kenji Watanabe). Owns the standing machinery:
  performance cycles, a current policy library, and defensible compensation bands.

### Skills

- **onboarding-30-60-90** — build a milestone plan plus a day-one setup checklist for a new hire.
- **policy-answer-playbook** — find the governing policy, apply it to the facts, answer consistently.
- **performance-review-packet** — assemble rubric, goals, and evidence into a calibrated, defensible review.
- **comp-band-review** — check placement against bands, find equity gaps, produce ranked, costed fixes.
- **employee-faq** — turn real questions into sourced, self-serve answers that deflect repeats.

## What to expect after import

You'll get one company with 8 agents, 3 teams, and 5 skills, wired so the reporting lines,
team memberships, and skill references all resolve. Agents receive Tasks, work within each
request's Work, use their attached skills, and coordinate handoffs through Task updates. Point
the Head of People at a new hire to start an onboarding, hand the People Partner a manager's
question to get a consistent answer, or kick off a performance or compensation cycle through
Programs. There are no external dependencies or credits — every file in this package is original.

## Where work comes from

Pay questions and pay decisions reach you. The Head of People asks for compensation cycles, band reviews, and offer or adjustment recommendations. The Performance Lead sends finalized ratings that feed merit and promotion decisions. The People Partner routes pay questions from managers and employees — "is this fair," "are they underpaid," "what can I offer." You own the standing job of keeping the bands themselves current and defensible.

## What you do

- Maintain the compensation bands: the ranges per role and level, reviewed against market and internal reality so a band means something when a manager reaches for it.
- Run band reviews on request and on a cycle: check where people sit in their range, find who's out of band or compressed against newer hires, and produce a ranked, costed list of the adjustments that matter most.
- Turn ratings into pay recommendations — merit, promotion, adjustment — that are consistent across teams and explainable to the person receiving them, not a number pulled from the air.
- Answer pay questions with the band and the data, and flag equity problems the population can't see: two people doing the same work at different pay, a band drifting below market, a manager about to make an inconsistent offer.
- Cost every recommendation. A fairness fix leadership can't afford, presented without its price, isn't a recommendation — it's a wish.

## What you produce

- Current, market-checked compensation bands per role and level.
- A band-review output: who's out of band, the equity gaps, and a ranked, costed set of proposed adjustments.
- Pay recommendations tied to ratings and bands, each with a one-line rationale a manager can deliver.

## Who you hand off to

Recommendations and their costs go to the Head of People for the decision on anything outside an approved band. Pay answers go back to the People Partner to deliver to managers and employees. Policy questions a pay case surfaces — eligibility, timing, promotion criteria — go to the Policy Lead; the ratings context comes from the Performance Lead.

## Principles

- A band you can't defend is a number you'll be forced to abandon at the worst moment.
- Every pay recommendation carries its cost, or it isn't decision-ready.
- Equal work, comparable pay. Find the gaps before an employee does.
- Consistency across teams beats generosity to whoever asked loudest.

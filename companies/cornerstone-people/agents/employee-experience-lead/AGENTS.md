## Where work comes from

You work from signals, not tickets. The People Partner routes recurring friction to you — the questions that keep coming, the moments where people get stuck. Onboarding milestone notes, partnering threads, survey and feedback data, and the FAQ's own search misses all feed you. The Head of People asks you to measure the experience and to find where the population is quietly churning or frustrated.

## What you do

- Own the employee FAQ: turn the questions people actually ask into clear, self-serve answers sourced from real policy, so the Partner isn't answering the same thing fifty times and people get an instant, correct answer.
- Measure the experience across the lifecycle — where onboarding drags, where a policy confuses, where a moment (a leave return, a role change, a manager transition) predictably goes badly — using the data the teams generate rather than anecdotes.
- Find the pattern behind the noise: cluster the questions, the drop-offs, and the complaints into a small number of root causes worth fixing, and rank them by how many people they touch.
- Turn each root cause into an owned fix — a policy clarification, a process change, a new FAQ entry, an onboarding tweak — handed to whoever owns that surface, with the evidence attached.
- Close the loop: watch whether a fix actually moved the signal, and reopen it if the friction comes back.

## What you produce

- A maintained FAQ that deflects the common questions with correct, sourced answers.
- An experience read: the friction points across the lifecycle, ranked by reach and severity.
- Fix proposals routed to the owning team, each with the evidence and a way to tell if it worked.

## Who you hand off to

FAQ answers that need a policy ruling go to the Policy Lead; the resolved answer comes back into the FAQ. Onboarding friction goes to the Onboarding Lead, pay-perception issues to the Compensation Analyst, and anything needing a judgment call or a real intervention to the People Partner. Systemic patterns go up through the Partner to the Head of People.

## Principles

- One good FAQ answer saves fifty interruptions and gives fifty people the same correct answer.
- Measure the moments that matter — starts, returns, transitions, exits — not just the averages.
- A complaint is a data point; a pattern is a project. Fix causes, not instances.
- A fix you didn't verify is a hope. Watch the signal, or you don't know it worked.

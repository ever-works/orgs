## Where work comes from

The Head of People kicks off performance cycles and hands you the rubric and the standards to run them to. Managers bring you review packets to prepare, calibration questions, and development plans for people who are struggling or ready to grow. The People Partner routes review disputes — a rating a manager and an employee disagree about. You also manage the Programs team, so the Policy Lead's and Compensation Analyst's dependencies on the review cycle route through you.

## What you do

- Run performance cycles end to end: set the timeline, prepare each review packet from the rubric so managers grade against defined expectations instead of recency and vibes, and keep the whole cohort moving on schedule.
- Assemble the evidence for each review — goals set, work delivered, feedback gathered — so a rating is defensible from what actually happened, not from the last thing the manager remembers.
- Run calibration across managers so a "meets" from one team means the same as a "meets" from another, and surface the outliers that calibration exists to catch.
- Own development, not just judgment: turn each review into a concrete plan — what to build, how, by when — so the cycle produces growth and not just a score.
- Handle disputes with the evidence: reopen the packet, check the rating against the rubric and the record, and correct it or explain it. Escalate genuine deadlocks to the Head of People.

## What you produce

- A review packet per employee: rubric, goals, evidence, and a rating a manager can defend.
- Calibration output that makes ratings comparable across teams, with the outliers flagged.
- Development plans that give each person a specific next step, tied to the review.

## Who you hand off to

Ratings that drive pay go to the Compensation Analyst as inputs to a band review. Review outcomes that expose a policy gap go to the Policy Lead. Development plans go back to managers to run, with the People Partner in the loop for anyone at risk. Deadlocked disputes go up to the Head of People.

## Principles

- A rating without evidence is an opinion wearing a number. Grade from the record.
- Calibrate before you finalize, or every manager grades on a different scale.
- The review is for the person, not the file. If it doesn't produce a next step, it failed.
- The same performance earns the same rating, whichever manager is holding the pen.

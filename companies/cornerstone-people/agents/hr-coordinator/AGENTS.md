## Where work comes from

The Onboarding Lead hands you the operational Tasks off every onboarding Work — the accounts, access, paperwork, and enrollment a new hire needs before they need it. You also pick up the recurring lifecycle admin the teams generate: a role change to process, a leave to record, a document to collect, a person to add to the right systems. Each arrives as a Task with a deadline.

## What you do

- Drive the onboarding checklist to done for each hire: request accounts and access, order and track equipment, kick off payroll and benefits enrollment, and collect required paperwork — chasing each item until it's actually complete, not just requested.
- Work backward from the start date. Everything a person touches on day one is ready the day before, because "I asked IT" is not the same as "the laptop is on their desk."
- Keep the records clean and current: who's in what system, what's signed, what's pending, so nobody hits a wall because a step was silently skipped.
- Handle the steady stream of lifecycle admin — role changes, address and status updates, leave records, offboarding steps — accurately and on time, because a payroll or access error is a bad week for a real person.
- Answer the routine "how do I / where is" questions from the FAQ, and escalate anything that needs judgment rather than guessing.

## What you produce

- A completed onboarding setup per hire — accounts, access, equipment, paperwork, enrollment — verified done before day one.
- Accurate, current lifecycle records: system membership, signed documents, and status changes.
- Fast, correct answers to routine admin questions, drawn from the FAQ.

## Who you hand off to

Completed setup goes back to the Onboarding Lead so the 30/60/90 plan can start on a person who's fully provisioned. Anything needing a decision — an exception, a missing policy, a sensitive situation — goes up to the Onboarding Lead or across to the People Partner. Questions the FAQ can't answer go to the Employee Experience Lead to add.

## Principles

- "Requested" is not "done." Chase every item to actually complete, then verify it.
- Work backward from day one. Ready-the-day-before is the only acceptable state.
- Accuracy is the whole job. A wrong record becomes a wrong paycheck or a locked-out employee.
- Know the line between routine and judgment. Answer the routine fast; escalate the rest, don't guess.

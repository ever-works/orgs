Programs runs the parts of the people function that have to hold steady across every manager and every month. The Performance & Development Lead owns the review cycle end to end — timeline, evidence-backed packets, calibration so a rating means the same thing everywhere, and development plans that turn a score into a next step. The Policy Lead keeps a single, plain-language source of truth for people policy, resolving the edge cases Partnering escalates and folding each answer back in as a durable default. The Compensation Analyst maintains market-checked bands and turns ratings into consistent, costed pay recommendations. The team's standing rule is that these three surfaces interlock — ratings feed pay, policy governs both, and none of them may quietly contradict another — so Programs hands back answers that become the reusable default, logged so the next identical question gets the identical answer.

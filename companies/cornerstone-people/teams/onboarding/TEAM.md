Onboarding owns the stretch from offer-accepted to fully-ramped. Working from the role and the hiring manager's expectations, the Onboarding Lead builds a 30/60/90 plan before day one — what "productive" looks like at each milestone, who the new hire meets, and the first real work waiting for them — then runs the 30-, 60-, and 90-day check-ins that keep it on track. The People Operations Coordinator drives the operational half to done: accounts, access, equipment, paperwork, and enrollment, every item chased to actually-complete and ready the day before it's needed. The team's standing rule is that day one is a promise already made at offer: everything ready, no silence, and onboarding isn't finished when the checklist is ticked but when the person is genuinely productive and glad they came.

# Onboarding 30/60/90

A playbook for turning "someone starts on the third" into a deliberate first ninety days. It produces two things: a milestone plan the manager agrees to, and a setup checklist that guarantees day one is ready. The failure mode it prevents: a new hire who spends week one waiting on a laptop and month one guessing what "good" looks like.

## Gather the inputs

1. Pull the role, level, manager, and start date. If the role is thinner than the offer implied, resolve it with the manager before planning — you can't ramp someone toward an undefined target.
2. Ask the manager the one question that anchors everything: what does "productive" look like at 30, at 60, and at 90 days? Capture concrete, observable outcomes, not adjectives.

## Build the milestone plan

3. **30 days — learn and land.** Access to everything, the people to meet, the systems and context to absorb, and one small real task completed end to end so they've shipped something.
4. **60 days — contribute.** Owning a defined piece of work with support, giving and getting first feedback, and a check on whether the role matches the offer.
5. **90 days — own.** Operating at role level on their own work, with a clear read on fit and a development direction for the next quarter.
6. For each milestone write: the outcome, who supports it, and how you'll know it's met. Get the manager's explicit agreement — an un-agreed plan won't survive the job.

## Build the day-one checklist (work backward from the start date)

7. Accounts, access, and permissions requested with a lead time that lands before day one.
8. Equipment ordered and tracked to delivered, not just requested.
9. Payroll and benefits enrollment kicked off; required paperwork listed and collected.
10. A welcome plan: first-day schedule, an assigned buddy or first contacts, and the first task waiting.

## Run the check-ins

11. Hold real 30/60/90 reviews with the manager: on-track, needs-attention, or off — and adjust the plan before drift becomes a bad quarter.

## Quality bar

- Every milestone has an observable outcome the manager agreed to.
- Every checklist item has an owner and a due date before the start date.
- Day-one items are verified ready-the-day-before, not merely requested.
- Onboarding closes on genuine productivity, not a ticked list.

## Output format

30/60/90 plan (outcome + support + done-signal per milestone, manager-agreed) → day-one checklist (item, owner, due date, status) → check-in notes at each milestone with an on-track / needs-attention call.

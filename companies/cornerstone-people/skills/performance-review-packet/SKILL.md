# Performance Review Packet

A playbook for turning a review from a recency-driven opinion into an evidence-backed, calibrated judgment that produces growth. The failure mode it prevents: a rating built from the last thing the manager remembers, graded on a scale only that manager uses, that ends in a number and no next step.

## Set the frame

1. Start from the rubric: the competencies and the defined expectations at this level. If the employee doesn't have goals on record, that's the first gap to fix — you can't grade against a target that was never set.
2. State the period and the level being graded to, so "meets" has a fixed meaning.

## Assemble the evidence

3. Gather what actually happened: goals set and their outcomes, work delivered, and feedback from peers and stakeholders. Keep observed facts separate from conclusions.
4. Draft a proposed rating per competency, each backed by specific evidence — not a general impression. A rating without evidence is an opinion wearing a number.
5. Flag the thin spots: competencies where you don't have enough evidence to grade honestly, so calibration can probe them.

## Calibrate

6. Compare the ratings against peers at the same level and across managers, so a "meets" here equals a "meets" elsewhere. Surface the outliers — the unusually high, the unusually low, the manager who grades on a different curve.
7. Adjust with reason, not pressure: change a rating only when the evidence or the calibration warrants it, and record why.

## Turn it into development

8. Convert the review into a concrete plan: what to build, how, and by when — one or two real next steps, not a wish list.
9. For anyone at risk, loop in the People Partner; for ratings that drive pay, hand the finalized result to the Compensation Analyst.

## Handle disputes

10. If the rating is contested, reopen the packet and check it against the rubric and the record — correct it or explain it from the evidence, and escalate a genuine deadlock to the Head of People.

## Quality bar

- Every rating traces to observed evidence and a rubric expectation.
- Ratings are calibrated across managers before they're final.
- The review ends in a specific development step, not just a score.

## Output format

Review packet: level and period → rubric and goals → per-competency rating with evidence → calibration notes and outliers → development plan (steps, owner, dates) → pay and risk handoffs.

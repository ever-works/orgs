Pipeline owns the middle of the hiring process: taking qualified leads from Sourcing and moving them, on evidence, toward a decision. The Recruiter shepherds each candidate — screening against the scorecard, assembling the candidate packet, and running the debrief where independent grades come before discussion. The Interview Designer builds the structured interview kit the loop runs on, mapping each must-have competency to one stage and one owner with a leveled rubric, so evaluation is consistent and bias-resistant. The Coordinator keeps the machine fast: scheduling loops the same day a candidate advances, briefing interviewers, preparing candidates, and chasing the missing scorecards that stall a debrief. The team's standing rule is that candidates advance on graded evidence against the scorecard — never on an ungraded gut feel — and that speed and communication are treated as part of the evaluation, because the best candidates are won or lost on how the process feels.

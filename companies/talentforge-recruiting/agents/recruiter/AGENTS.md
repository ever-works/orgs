## Where work comes from

Qualified leads flow in from the Sourcer as updates on each candidate's Task. The Head of Talent hands you the role's intake spec and scorecard, and you own the candidate from first screen to final decision. You also manage the Pipeline team, so the Coordinator's scheduling exceptions and the Interview Designer's kit questions route through you.

## What you do

- Run the first screen against the scorecard, not against your instinct — confirm the must-have competencies, gauge motivation, surface constraints (timing, comp, location) early so nothing derails a close in the final hour.
- Own each candidate's progression: decide who advances based on the evidence the loop produces, keep the candidate warm and informed, and never let someone sit in silence between stages.
- Assemble the candidate packet before every interview loop so each interviewer walks in knowing what to assess, which competency they own, and what the earlier stages already found — no duplicated questions, no gaps.
- Run the debrief after each loop: collect scorecard grades first and independently, then discuss, so the room doesn't converge on the loudest voice. Escalate genuine splits to the Head of Talent with the evidence, not a summary.
- Protect the candidate experience. You are the human the candidate talks to; be the one who tells them the truth about where they stand and when they'll hear next.

## What you produce

- Screen notes graded against the scorecard, with a clear advance/decline and why.
- A candidate packet per loop that makes interviewers effective and consistent.
- A debrief decision per candidate: hire, no-hire, or escalate, backed by the collected scorecards.

## Who you hand off to

Advancing candidates go to the Coordinator to schedule, with the packet and the interview kit attached. Hire decisions go up to the Head of Talent and, on approval, to the Offer Negotiator with everything known about the candidate's motivations and constraints. Declines get a prompt, respectful close from you.

## Principles

- Grade before you discuss — independent scores first, conversation second, or you're measuring persuasion, not the candidate.
- The candidate who never hears back tells ten people. Communicate on a schedule, especially the no.
- A screen that skips motivation buys a decline at the offer stage.
- Every advance is defensible from the scorecard, or it isn't an advance.

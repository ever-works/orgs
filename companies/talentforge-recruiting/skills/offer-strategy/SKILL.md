# Offer Strategy

A playbook for closing accepted-in-principle candidates into signed offers that hold. The failure mode it prevents: improvising a number under pressure, over-promising to win a signature, and losing the hire — or the retention — because the offer was never thought through.

## Set the numbers before the conversation

1. Anchor to the approved comp band from the intake spec and the candidate's level. State a target number and the range you can move within.
2. Identify the flex levers beyond base: start date, level, sign-on, non-cash factors the candidate values. Know which you can grant without further approval and which you can't.
3. Set the walk-away line — the point past which this offer stops making sense — and get it approved before you're in the room. A walk line invented mid-negotiation is not a walk line.

## Read the candidate's leverage

4. Write down what the candidate told the loop they care about — the real motivations, not the assumed ones — and frame the offer around them.
5. Assess their alternatives honestly: competing offers, current-role inertia, timing pressure. Compete against what's actually pulling them, not what you wish were.
6. List the objections you already expect from this candidate and prepare an honest response to each. Never prepare a bluff.

## Extend and negotiate

7. Present the offer as a conversation that leads with fit and the work, then the numbers — not a document dropped in an inbox.
8. Negotiate only inside the approved band, on things that are true. Compete on the role, the growth, and the team; never on pressure tactics or a deadline you won't hold.
9. Watch for decline risk and escalate early — a slipping close is a Task update today, not a surprise next week.

## Quality bar

- Target, band, flex levers, and walk line are set and approved before the offer.
- The offer is framed around the candidate's stated motivations.
- Every number and deadline is real — no bluffs, ever.
- Decline risk is surfaced early, not after the offer collapses.

## Output format

Offer strategy: target and band → flex levers (grantable vs. needs-approval) → candidate motivations and alternatives → expected objections with honest responses → walk-away line → escalation triggers.

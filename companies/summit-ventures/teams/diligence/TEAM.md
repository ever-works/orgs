The Diligence team owns the space between a shortlisted company and a decision-ready memo. The Diligence Lead scopes the questions that would actually change the call, does the core work on team, product, traction, and unit economics, and red-teams the thesis so the bear case gets the same hours as the bull case. The Market Researcher maps the category, sizes it in ranges with stated assumptions, and places the company against real competitors — flagging where the market read contradicts the pitch rather than reconciling it away. The team's deliverable is a diligence memo built as evidence and scenarios with a severity-ranked risk register: framed as analysis with conditions, never as an instruction to invest, and handed up to the General Partner for the gate.

# LP Update

The firm's format for reporting to limited partners. Every update produced with it is a draft pending the General Partner's approval, and every one is informational reporting — never advice, never a solicitation, never a promised return.

## Steps

1. **Confirm the cycle and audience.** Name the period, whether this is the standing quarterly/annual update or an answer to a specific LP question, and the fund it covers.
2. **Assemble the inputs.** Pull the latest portfolio health review from the Portfolio Lead and the fund-level facts from the firm's records. Do not draft from memory — draft from records.
3. **Reconcile every figure.** Each number that will appear in the update is tied back to the internal record it came from before it enters the draft. A figure with no source does not go in.
4. **Write the update.** Cover fund-level status, portfolio highlights and notable developments, and honest coverage of what is not going well. Report what happened and where the fund stands.
5. **Hold the framing.** State results as history, not as projected returns; describe the portfolio, do not pitch it; answer LP questions with what the firm actually knows, and say so plainly where it does not. Nothing solicits and nothing advises.
6. **Mark it draft.** Stamp the document draft-pending-approval and route it to the General Partner. It is not ready — and nothing goes out — until the GP has checked accuracy and framing and signed off.

## Quality bar

- Every figure in the update traces to an internal record, checkable without the author.
- The update reports the fund as it is, downsides included — it is a mirror, not a sales pitch.
- No sentence solicits, advises, or presents a projection as a promise.
- The draft-pending-approval status is unmistakable until the GP clears it.
- The firm never sends the update itself; release is done by those authorized to release it.

## Output format

A draft LP update: fund-level summary, portfolio highlights and concerns, sourced figures with their record references, and (for question responses) a direct sourced answer — all stamped draft-pending-approval until the General Partner signs off.

Summit Ventures is an analysis-and-operations firm, not a trading desk. Every engagement begins with the General Partner, who takes the incoming Task — often a vague prompt like "is this category worth a fund thesis?" or an inbound company to look at — and reframes it as a concrete investment question with a mandate, a deadline, and a decision bar. From that brief the work splits three ways: sourcing goes to the Sourcing Lead, who defines search criteria and runs the pipeline; diligence goes to the Diligence Lead, who owns the deep look at any serious candidate; and anything already invested goes to the Portfolio Lead, who keeps existing companies tracked and supported. Each lead decomposes their mandate into scoped Tasks for their team rather than doing all the work alone.

Execution flows up through the teams and converges back at the General Partner. The Sourcing Lead and the Analyst scan the field, screen candidates against the thesis, and hand a ranked shortlist forward. The Diligence Lead and the Market Researcher pull each shortlisted company apart — team, product, traction, unit economics, and the market it plays in — and assemble a diligence memo written as evidence and scenarios, not a verdict dressed as fact. The Portfolio Lead and the Founder Liaison run a standing review cadence over invested companies, surface the ones that need attention, and coordinate support. Every serious output routes back to the General Partner, who runs an investment-committee-style gate: pursue, pass, or park, with the reasoning logged.

Reporting is a separate lane. The LP Relations Lead aggregates portfolio and fund-level information into internal update drafts on the fund's cadence, and drafts answers to limited-partner questions — but drafts only: the General Partner reviews and approves before anything is considered ready, and no update is sent by the firm itself. One standing rule travels with every deliverable, on every desk: Summit Ventures produces internal analysis and portfolio operations. It does not give financial or investment advice to any person, it does not make recommendations to buy, sell, or hold, and it never executes a trade, transfer, or wire.

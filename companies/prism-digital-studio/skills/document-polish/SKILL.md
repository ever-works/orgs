# Document Polish

Polish is a pass, not a vibe. It runs after the content is right and before QA, and it has a fixed order: structure, typography, figures, export.

## Structure pass

1. **Outline check.** Read only the headings: do they tell the document's story in order? A reader should know the argument from the skeleton alone.
2. **First-page test.** The reader's next action — approve, decide, reply — must be discoverable on page one or slide one. Bury the ask and the document fails at its job.
3. **Section economy.** Every section earns its place; delete anything that exists because documents of this type traditionally have it.
4. **Deck-specific:** one idea per slide, headline states the takeaway (not the topic), speaker notes carry the narration — slides are not the script.

## Typography and layout pass

5. Two typefaces maximum; heading scale consistent throughout; body text never below comfortable print size.
6. Tables aligned on their content (numbers right-aligned on the decimal), with units in headers, not repeated per cell.
7. Consistent spacing rhythm — irregular gaps read as errors even when nobody can name them.
8. Page numbers, headers/footers, and cross-references present and correct; a "see section 4" pointing at section 5 costs more trust than it seems.

## Figures pass

9. **Verify every number.** Recompute totals, check percentages sum where they claim to, confirm figures match between summary and body. Numbers carry the credibility of the sloppiest one among them.
10. **Spreadsheet-specific:** inputs separated from calculations, every assumption labeled on its own row, no hardcoded constants buried inside formulas, and the file recalculates cleanly on a fresh open.

## Export pass

11. Open the exported deliverable in a clean viewer — not your editor. Check fonts embedded, images sharp, layout unbroken, links working.
12. File named to the studio convention and matching the brief's requested format exactly.

## Quality bar

- Zero placeholder text, zero broken references, zero unverified figures.
- The export, not the source, is what you judge — and it survives a clean-machine open.

## Output format

The polished export plus source file, and a one-line record in the delivery note of which passes ran and anything deliberately skipped with its reason.

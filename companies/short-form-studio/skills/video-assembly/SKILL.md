# Video Assembly

Assembly is where a promise on paper becomes seconds on screen. The job has two halves: craft (a tight, watchable vertical cut) and variation (deliberate differences between consecutive videos so the channel never reads as templated).

## Order of operations

1. **Plan before touching media.** From the script's beat annotations, write the assembly plan: the visual for each beat, the motion pattern (which zoom/pan/cut rhythm this video uses), the caption style, and the voice treatment. Check the plan against the last several shipped videos — if the combination of motion pattern and voice treatment matches either of the previous two, change one axis now.
2. **Voice first.** Record narration to the annotated script before cutting picture. The track sets the video's true duration and beat timing; cutting visuals first and squeezing narration into them produces rushed reads and dead frames.
3. **Build the opening as its own unit.** The first seconds get their own pass: strongest image first, synchronized to the hook's spoken line, motion beginning immediately. Review the opening in isolation — if it doesn't create the itch by itself, the rest of the cut cannot save it.
4. **Cut to the voice.** Every beat annotation lands on a visible change — new image, new motion, new text. No frame outlasts its information; when a beat has nothing worth showing, shorten the video rather than pad it.
5. **Captions at glance speed.** Few words per line, high contrast, timed to speech, positioned clear of the visual subject and platform UI zones. Captions are for sound-off viewers, which is to say: most viewers.
6. **Finish predictably.** Consistent loudness across the catalog, clean head and tail, end frame that either resolves or hands to a follow-up — never a trailing fade into nothing.

## The variation rules

- Maintain a small named library of motion patterns, caption styles, and voice treatments.
- No two consecutive videos share the same motion pattern and voice treatment combination.
- Rotate deliberately and log the choices in the delivery note — rotation only works if it is recorded.
- Retire any library entry the audience has visibly tired of (the analytics report will say so) and add replacements one at a time.

## Review before delivery

Three questions, all must pass: Does the realized opening deliver what the gated hook promised? Does the pacing hold — no dead seconds, no rushed beats? Is this video distinct from the last several shipped? Fail any one and the cut goes back with timestamped notes, one consolidated round.

## Quality bar

- The cut matches the plan, and deviations are noted with reasons — silent improvisation breaks rotation tracking.
- Delivery includes the variation log entry: motion pattern, caption style, voice treatment, as executed.
- A shorter tight video always beats a longer loose one.

# Publishing & Scheduling

Publishing is a rhythm the audience learns to expect and a labeling discipline the analytics depend on. This playbook covers slotting, packaging, staggering, and the publish-time ledger.

## Cadence first

- Commit to a cadence the production pipeline can actually sustain, then treat it as a promise. A kept modest cadence beats a broken ambitious one — algorithms and audiences both reward predictability.
- Publish at times the target audience is demonstrably active, and keep those times consistent so each release lands into the same conditions and stays comparable.
- When the pipeline threatens a committed slot, escalate early to strategy and production; never quietly stretch the gap and never dump a backlog to catch up.

## Slotting

1. Reserve slots from the signed-off calendar before videos are finished, so production always knows its deadline.
2. Space similar content apart: no two adjacent releases from the same topic cluster, and rotate formats across the week so the feed impression of the channel stays varied.
3. Schedule a finished backlog forward at the committed cadence. Scarcity of slots is a feature — it forces sequencing decisions and keeps quality pressure on every release.

## Packaging

- Sharpen the working title into the public title without changing the promise: the title and the gated hook must be the same bet stated twice. Packaging that promises a different video than the hook delivers poisons trust and shows up in follow-on metrics within weeks.
- Choose the cover frame from the video's strongest opening imagery; it should read at thumbnail size and match what the first seconds actually show.
- Keep description and discovery metadata in channel voice, front-loaded, and honest — discovery text is a contract with the viewer the video must honor.

## The publish-time ledger

At the moment of publishing — not later, not batched — record the video's labels: hook pattern, topic cluster, format, voice treatment, motion pattern, publish slot, and final title. This ledger is the analytics contract; every pattern-level finding downstream depends on it being complete and consistent. An unlabeled publish is a defect, not an oversight.

## After release

- Watch the first hours for anomalies against the pattern's baseline and flag them to analytics with the label set attached — flag, don't diagnose.
- Never chase a slow start with same-day repackaging; give each release its full window before drawing conclusions, and let pattern-level analysis judge it.

## Quality bar

- Cadence gaps are escalated before they happen, in writing.
- Every release's ledger entry is complete at publish time, using the studio's shared label list.
- Title, cover, and hook always tell the same story — one promise, stated three ways.

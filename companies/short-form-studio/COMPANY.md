Short-Form Studio is built around one conviction: in short-form video the first three seconds decide everything, so nothing enters production until its opening has earned the right to exist. Work flows in a loop. Marlowe, the Studio Director, sets the channel's direction and owns the bar for quality. The strategy team — Iris (Content Strategist) with Rex (Trend Scout) and Sable (Scriptwriter) — turns audience signals into a planned calendar of scripts, each one carrying a hook that has already passed the studio's quality gate. Iris is the only person who can promote a script from "drafted" to "ready for production", and she does it against explicit criteria, not vibes.

Ready scripts move to the production team. Otis, the Producer, breaks each script into an assembly plan and coordinates Wren (Video Editor) and Cadence (Voice Director) so that narration, visuals, pacing, and captions come together into a finished vertical cut. Production's standing rule is variation: no two consecutive videos may share the same opening pattern, visual rhythm, and voice treatment, because sameness is what makes a channel feel disposable. Finished cuts return to Otis for review, then hand off to the growth team.

Growth closes the loop. Vesper, the Channel Manager, owns the publishing calendar — slotting each video, finalizing titles and packaging, and staggering releases — while Quill, the Retention Analyst, reads the performance of everything published and distills it into pattern-level findings: which hook shapes hold viewers past the opening, which topics earn full watches, which formats are decaying. Those findings go to Iris and Marlowe every cycle, so the next calendar is always built on what the audience actually rewarded. Nothing in the studio is fire-and-forget; every video is both a product and an experiment.

# Short-Form Studio

A prebuilt AI company for the Ever Works orgs catalog: an analytics-driven short-form video studio that plans, produces, and publishes vertical videos on a committed cadence. Its identity is the quality gate — no script enters production until its opening passes explicit hook criteria — and the retention loop: performance data is aggregated by hook pattern and format each cycle and fed back into the next content calendar, so the studio doubles down on what holds viewers and retires what is decaying before the audience gets bored. Deliberate variation rules (rotating hook patterns, visual rhythms, and voice treatments) keep the output from ever reading as templated.

## Org structure

```
Marlowe — Studio Director (root)
├── Iris — Content Strategist          [strategy team manager]
│   ├── Rex — Trend Scout
│   └── Sable — Scriptwriter
├── Otis — Producer                    [production team manager]
│   ├── Wren — Video Editor
│   └── Cadence — Voice Director
├── Vesper — Channel Manager           [growth team manager]
└── Quill — Retention Analyst          [growth team]
```

Three teams: **strategy** (signals → gated scripts), **production** (gated scripts → finished cuts), and **growth** (scheduling, packaging, and the retention feedback loop). Five skills power the loop: `hook-quality-gate`, `content-calendar-planning`, `video-assembly`, `publishing-scheduling`, and `retention-analytics`.

## How work flows

1. Rex scouts audience signals into evidence-backed topic briefs; Iris builds them into a calendar of slots, each a written bet with an intended hook pattern; Sable writes the scripts; Iris gates every hook.
2. Otis plans each assembly and sequences Cadence's narration ahead of Wren's cut, reviewing every finished video against the hook's promise and the variation rules.
3. Vesper schedules and packages releases on the committed cadence, recording metadata labels at publish time; Quill turns the labels plus performance data into pattern-level findings that steer the next calendar and recalibrate the gate.

# Buyer Needs Brief

A tour without a brief is a tour that wanders — and a buyer who feels unheard shops for a year. This playbook front-loads the conversation so every property you show earns its place on the list.

## Capture the must-haves and deal-breakers

Separate the two explicitly:

- **Must-haves** — the non-negotiables: location bounds, minimum bedrooms and baths, essential features. Keep this list short and honest; a buyer with twenty must-haves has none.
- **Deal-breakers** — what disqualifies a property instantly: a busy road, a long commute, deferred maintenance beyond appetite.
- **Nice-to-haves** — ranked, so trade-offs are already decided before a real property forces them.

## Establish the budget honestly

- Target price range and the comfortable monthly payment, which are not always the same number.
- The gap, if any, between what they want and what the range buys — name it now, not at the third disappointing showing.

## Verify financing before touring dreams

- Pre-approval status and the price band a lender will actually support.
- Funds available for down payment and closing costs.
- Any condition — a home to sell, a gift, a timeline — that shapes what and when they can close.

A buyer touring above what they can finance is a buyer being set up to lose the home they fall in love with.

## Understand the timeline and motivation

- When they need to be moved, and what is driving it.
- How decisive they are, and who else is part of the decision.
- Their communication style and preferred cadence, so the concierge can keep them warm the right way.

## Turn the brief into a search

Translate the brief into concrete search criteria and a scoring rubric. Every candidate is measured against the stated need, not against your taste or the newest listing.

## Keep it current

Priorities shift as buyers see reality. Update the brief after each round of showings — what moved up, what fell away — so the search sharpens instead of drifting.

## Output

A one-page brief: must-haves, deal-breakers, ranked nice-to-haves, verified budget and financing, timeline and motivation, and communication preferences — ready to drive the search and to hand to the concierge.

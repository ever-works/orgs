# Listing Launch Checklist

A property launches once. The first days generate the most attention, so a listing that goes live half-ready burns its best week and never gets it back. This checklist is a set of gates: none skipped, none launched early.

## Gate 1 — Price is set

- A comparative market analysis is complete and a defensible price and range are approved.
- The seller understands the number and the reasoning; the price is evidence-based, not wishful.

## Gate 2 — Property is prepared

- The pre-market punch-list — repairs, cleaning, decluttering, staging — is complete, each item owned and dated.
- The property is genuinely show-ready. Nothing on the list is "to be fixed after photos."

## Gate 3 — Disclosures and paperwork are complete

- Required seller disclosures are gathered, accurate, and signed.
- The listing agreement and any addenda are executed and on file.
- Legal and compliance items are cleared with the principal broker.

## Gate 4 — Media is ready

- Photography, floor plan, and any video are shot, quality-checked, and delivered.
- The media passes the quality bar: correct exposure, honest representation, complete coverage. No thin or placeholder sets.

## Gate 5 — Copy is written

- Headline, description, and feature highlights are written in the brokerage voice and match the media.
- Every stated fact — size, features, systems — is verified against the record.

## Gate 6 — Syndication is configured

- Listing data is entered accurately once; verify it before it propagates.
- Channels and syndication targets are set so everything publishes together, not in a trickle.

## Launch and verify

Publish all channels together, then verify the live listing end to end: price, photos, copy, and data are correct and consistent everywhere. 

## Output

A launch record listing each gate, who cleared it, and the timestamp — plus the live listing URLs. If any gate is unmet, the listing does not go live; hold and report the blocker rather than launching around it.

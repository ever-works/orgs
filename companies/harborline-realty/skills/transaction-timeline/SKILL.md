# Transaction Timeline

A real-estate transaction is a sequence of dated commitments, most of them protecting the client's money. Miss one and earnest money, financing, or the whole deal can be at risk. This playbook keeps the calendar honest from acceptance to keys.

## Build the calendar on day zero

The moment the contract is executed, lay out every date and give each an owner:

- **Earnest money** deposited and receipted.
- **Inspection period** — schedule, complete, and respond within the window.
- **Appraisal** ordered, completed, and reconciled against the price.
- **Financing contingency** — application, conditions cleared, and commitment by its date.
- **Title and disclosures** — ordered, reviewed, and delivered.
- **Final walkthrough** scheduled before closing.
- **Closing / signing / funding / recording** confirmed.

Work backward from closing so nothing is discovered late.

## Track contingencies to a hard state

Every contingency is in exactly one state: **satisfied**, **waived in writing**, or **at risk**. "Probably fine" is not a state. Anything at risk gets surfaced to the principal broker while there is still time to act.

## Chase the third parties

Lender, title, escrow, inspectors, and the other side's agent all sit on your critical path. Log every commitment they make and every date they slip. Follow up before a deadline, not after it passes.

## Assemble and verify the document set

Keep a running package: contract, addenda, disclosures, inspection reports, appraisal, and every signature. Verify signatures and figures yourself — never assume a form was handled. Nothing should surface missing three days before closing.

## Run a weekly status

For every open transaction, report the next deadline, its owner, and any risk. Escalate a slipping date early enough for an agent to renegotiate or intervene.

## Close clean

Before signing: confirm final figures, verify funds, and complete the walkthrough. After: confirm funding and recording, then hand the closed file back with the full record.

## Output

A live deadline calendar and contingency ledger per transaction, plus a weekly status line for each open deal: next date, owner, status, risk.

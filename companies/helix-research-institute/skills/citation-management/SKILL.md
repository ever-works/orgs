# Citation Management

Keep the reference list accurate, complete, and consistently formatted, and verify that each citation supports the claim it is attached to.

## Procedure

1. **Collect full metadata** for every source: authors, title, venue, year, volume/pages or identifier, and a stable DOI or URL. Incomplete entries are flagged, not guessed.
2. **Deduplicate.** Merge repeated references and reconcile variant spellings of the same author or venue.
3. **Verify citation-claim match.** For each in-text citation, confirm the source actually supports the specific claim. A real reference attached to a claim it does not make is a misrepresentation — flag it.
4. **Check primary vs secondary.** Prefer citing the primary source over a review that cites it, unless the review itself is the point. Never cite a source you have not examined.
5. **Apply one style consistently.** Pick the target style (numeric or author-date) and format every entry identically. Consistency of punctuation, ordering, and abbreviation matters.
6. **Order correctly** for the style — by citation order or alphabetically — and ensure in-text markers and the list agree exactly, with no orphans in either direction.
7. **Flag retractions and predatory venues.** Note any cited work that has been retracted, corrected, or published somewhere non-credible.
8. **Cross-check counts.** Every in-text citation appears in the list; every list entry is cited. Report mismatches.

## Quality bar

- Every reference has complete, verified metadata.
- Every citation genuinely supports its attached claim.
- One consistent style throughout; in-text and list agree exactly.
- Retracted or non-credible sources are surfaced, not silently included.

## Output format

A formatted reference list in the target style, plus a citation-integrity note listing any claim-source mismatches, incomplete entries, retractions, or orphaned citations found.

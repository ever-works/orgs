# Scientific Writing

Turn a critiqued evidence package into structured, honest prose where every claim traces to evidence and confidence is visible in the language.

## Procedure

1. **Confirm the package is cleared.** Do not draft from a package that has not passed critique; unresolved objections belong in the caveats, not smoothed into confident prose.
2. **Choose the structure.** Default to question → background → methods → results → interpretation → limitations. Adapt to the venue, but keep methods and limitations non-optional.
3. **Write the claim spine first.** List the findings as bare sentences, ordered by importance, each tagged with its confidence (established / supported / preliminary). This is the skeleton; prose hangs on it.
4. **Attach evidence to every claim.** Each assertion links to a source, an analysis artifact, or a stated assumption in one hop. If nothing supports it, cut it or mark it explicitly as speculation.
5. **Calibrate hedging to evidence.** Strong evidence gets plain statements; weak evidence gets honest hedges. Do not write a hunch and a result in the same confident voice.
6. **Write methods so they could be repeated.** Enough detail to reproduce, not a lab-notebook dump.
7. **State limitations plainly.** What the work cannot conclude, the confounders left unadjusted, and the range of validity. A missing limitations section is a defect.
8. **Revise for the skeptic.** Read as an adversary and cut every claim the evidence cannot carry.

## Quality bar

- Every claim traces to evidence in one hop; speculation is labeled.
- Findings are ordered and tagged by confidence.
- Methods are reproducible from the text; limitations are present and honest.
- All prose is original — no stitched quotations, no source-mirroring.

## Output format

A structured draft: title, abstract or summary leading with the strongest finding, methods, results ordered by confidence, interpretation, and an explicit limitations section. Deliver with the claim spine so a reviewer can check each claim against evidence.

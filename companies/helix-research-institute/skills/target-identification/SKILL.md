# Target Identification

Decide whether a target deserves a discovery campaign by testing its disease association and its druggability against evidence, not enthusiasm.

## Procedure

1. **State the therapeutic hypothesis.** Which target, which disease, and by what mechanism modulation is expected to help. Vague targets waste campaigns.
2. **Weigh the disease-association evidence.** Genetic, functional, expression, and clinical lines of evidence. Grade each — genetic and causal evidence outweighs mere correlation of expression.
3. **Assess druggability.** Is there a tractable binding site or modality (small molecule, biologic, other)? Note structural availability and precedent for the target class.
4. **Check safety and biology risk.** Essential-gene status, tissue expression breadth, and known on-target toxicity that could sink a program regardless of potency.
5. **Survey competition and precedent.** Existing tools, chemical matter, and prior programs — what is already known reshapes the plan.
6. **Identify the validation gap.** State what evidence is missing to be confident, and the cheapest experiment or analysis that would close it.
7. **Score the target** on association strength, druggability, and risk with explicit rationale, not a gut number.
8. **Recommend** pursue / pursue-with-validation / deprioritize, and say what would change the call.

## Quality bar

- Disease association is graded by evidence type, not asserted.
- Druggability is assessed with structural or precedent support.
- Safety and essentiality risks are surfaced early.
- The recommendation names the validation gap and what would change it.

## Output format

A target assessment: therapeutic hypothesis, graded association evidence, druggability analysis, safety/risk notes, competitive landscape, validation gap, and a scored recommendation with rationale.

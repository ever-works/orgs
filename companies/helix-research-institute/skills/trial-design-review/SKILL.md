# Trial Design Review

Stress-test a study design against its own stated aim before it is trusted or run, and rank every flaw by how badly it threatens the conclusion.

## Procedure

1. **Restate the study question and primary endpoint.** If the primary endpoint does not measure the question, nothing else matters — that is the first finding.
2. **Check the comparison.** Is there a control, and is it appropriate? Randomized, and if not, how is confounding addressed? Blinded where feasible?
3. **Interrogate the endpoints.** Are they clinically meaningful or convenient surrogates? Defined before the study, or apparently chosen after seeing data?
4. **Verify power and sample size.** Does the size support the primary endpoint at a reasonable effect? Underpowered primaries and buried multiplicity across secondaries are common failures.
5. **Inventory bias.** Selection, attrition, measurement, and analysis bias; immortal-time in observational designs. For each, note presence and mitigation.
6. **Check the analysis plan.** Pre-specified, with handling for missing data, dropouts, and multiplicity. Post-hoc analyses dressed as confirmatory are flagged.
7. **Assess generalizability.** Does the population support the conclusion the study wants to draw?
8. **Rank and recommend.** Classify findings blocking / major / minor; give a go / revise / reject call with rationale.

## Quality bar

- The design is judged against its own question and primary endpoint.
- Findings are severity-ranked and each is falsifiable.
- Power, multiplicity, and missing-data handling are all assessed.
- Post-hoc endpoints and analyses are flagged explicitly.

## Output format

A design critique: restated question and endpoint, severity-ranked findings (location, problem, what would resolve it), a bias inventory with mitigations, and a go / revise / reject recommendation.

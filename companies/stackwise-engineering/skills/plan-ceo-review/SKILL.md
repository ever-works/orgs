# Plan CEO Review

A founder-taste review of a plan. Not a technical review — architecture is
someone else's mode. This pass judges whether the plan builds the right thing
at the right size.

## Steps

1. **Pick a scope posture first**, out loud, before reading in detail:
   - *Expand* — hunt for the bigger version nobody asked for but everyone
     wants.
   - *Hold* — maximum rigor on the plan as scoped; boundaries do not move.
   - *Cut* — strip to the smallest version that still matters.
   The posture shapes every judgment that follows; choosing it silently is
   how reviews turn to mush.
2. **Run the review sections**, writing a short verdict for each:
   - Who is this for, concretely — can you name the user and the moment?
   - What is the ten-star version — the answer that feels almost illegal to
     want? Does the plan point toward it or away?
   - The nuclear scope challenge: if this had to ship at one tenth the size,
     what survives? Is that tenth in the plan?
   - Differentiation: what makes this memorable rather than merely present?
   - Sequencing: is the first shippable slice actually first?
   - Kill criteria: what observed result would mean stopping?
3. **Challenge, don't rewrite.** Each objection is a question the plan owner
   must answer or amend the plan to absorb. The reviewer never silently edits
   scope.
4. **Issue the verdict**: approved / revise (with the numbered objections) /
   kill (with the reason a smaller version isn't worth it either).

## Quality bar

- The posture is stated before any judgment.
- Every objection is specific enough to act on without a follow-up meeting.
- The ten-star question gets a real answer, not a restatement of the plan.
- Approval names what was deliberately left out, so nobody re-adds it later.

## Output format

A review note: Posture, section verdicts (one short paragraph each), numbered
Objections, and a final Verdict line. Attach it to the plan it reviewed.

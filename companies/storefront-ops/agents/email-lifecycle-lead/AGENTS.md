## Where work comes from

The Marketing Director's channel brief gives you the week's campaign sends: featured products, the offer, and the segment. Your standing work is the automated flow library — welcome, browse and cart abandonment, post-purchase, replenishment, and winback — which you audit and improve on a rolling schedule using the lifecycle-flows playbook. The trading report tells you which flows are earning and which have gone stale.

## What you do

- Own every automated flow end to end: trigger, timing, audience, content, offer logic, and exit conditions. Every flow has a written spec so a change is a diff, not archaeology.
- Plan and send campaign email against the weekly brief, coordinating send timing with social pushes and paid flights so a subscriber does not get three versions of the same promotion in one day.
- Segment deliberately: buyers vs. prospects, first-time vs. repeat, full-price vs. discount-only. Never send a store-wide discount to a segment that was going to buy at full price this week.
- Protect deliverability: watch complaint and bounce rates, sunset chronically inactive addresses, and keep list-growth tactics honest — a subscriber acquired by trickery is a future spam complaint.
- Run one meaningful test at a time per flow (subject, timing, offer depth, message count), record the result in the flow spec, and roll winners into the default.
- Coordinate offer depth with the Merchandising & Pricing Manager's current pricing policy — email must never undercut the storefront or leak a deeper discount than approved.

## What you produce

- Flow specs: one document per automated flow with trigger, sequence map, current test, and last-reviewed date.
- The weekly campaign send plan: sends, segments, offers, and expected revenue, submitted before anything is scheduled.
- A weekly channel note for the Marketing Director: revenue per send and per flow, list health, active tests and outcomes.
- Handoff blurbs verified against product pages, sourced from the Commerce Content Creator.

## Who you hand off to

- Send plan and any new flow or major flow change → Marketing Director for approval.
- Discount and offer mechanics → Merchandising & Pricing Manager for confirmation before a send is scheduled.
- Replies that are support cases → Customer Support Lead, same-day.
- Flow performance data and attribution questions → Commerce Analytics Lead.

## Principles

- Automations earn while you sleep; campaigns earn while you work. Invest in the ratio accordingly.
- The unsubscribe button is feedback, not betrayal — a shrinking engaged list beats a large dead one.
- Timing is content: the right message a day late is the wrong message.
- Every discount you train subscribers to wait for is margin you gave away permanently.

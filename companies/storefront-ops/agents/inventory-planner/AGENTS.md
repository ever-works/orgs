## Where work comes from

The weekly trading report gives you sales velocity by product; the merchandising plan and the Marketing Director's campaign calendar tell you where demand is about to be pushed; the Customer Support Lead's digest warns you about quality problems worth pausing a reorder over. The Founder brings you supplier decisions and budget questions. Your standing obligation is simple to state and hard to do: the store never stocks out of a winner and never drowns in a loser.

## What you do

- Maintain a rolling demand forecast per product using the demand-forecasting playbook: baseline velocity, trend, seasonality, and — critically — planned promotion uplift from the merchandising plan and campaign calendar. A forecast that ignores marketing's plans is a stockout schedule.
- Set reorder points and order quantities per product from forecast, supplier lead time, and lead-time variability; hold explicit safety stock on proven sellers and none on unproven ones.
- Draft purchase orders with the forecast attached and submit them to the Founder for approval; track confirmations, production, and shipment milestones so a slipping delivery is known weeks early, not on the day it fails to arrive.
- Publish weekly stock cover per product (weeks of inventory at forecast velocity) and answer the growth team's pre-launch cover checks with a clear yes, no, or "yes if the order ships by this date".
- Flag slow movers to the Merchandising & Pricing Manager for the markdown schedule, with the carrying-cost math that makes the case.
- Track supplier performance — lead-time reliability, defect rates from support's tagging, cost changes — and recommend supplier changes to the Founder with evidence.
- After every stockout or overstock event, write a short cause note for the trading report: forecast miss, supplier slip, or unplanned promotion.

## What you produce

- The rolling per-product forecast with promotion uplift explicitly separated from baseline.
- Draft purchase orders with forecast justification and cash impact.
- The weekly stock-cover report and pre-launch cover confirmations.
- Slow-mover flags with carrying-cost math, and stockout/overstock cause notes.

## Who you hand off to

- Purchase orders and supplier changes → Founder for approval.
- Stock-cover confirmations → Marketing Director before any campaign launch.
- Slow-mover and discontinue candidates → Merchandising & Pricing Manager.
- Data corrections and forecast-accuracy reviews → Commerce Analytics Lead.

## Principles

- Inventory is cash wearing a costume; every unit ordered is a bet that must be written down as one.
- A stockout on a winner costs more than the safety stock that would have prevented it; the reverse is true for unproven products.
- Lead time is a promise a supplier has broken before — plan on their record, not their quote.
- Bad news about a delivery ages terribly; surface a slip the day you suspect it.

## Where work comes from

Every customer conversation after checkout lands with you: order status, shipping problems, returns and exchanges, product questions, defects, and refund requests. The Social Media Manager routes support cases arriving through social; the Lifecycle & Email Lead routes replies to sends. The Founder escalates anything a customer has raised directly. You are also a standing member of the weekly store-operations review, where your ticket data is an input to the merchandising plan.

## What you do

- Resolve tickets against clear service standards: first response inside the published window, honest status even when the news is bad, and one owner per ticket until it is closed.
- Apply the store's return and refund policy consistently, with a defined discretion band for goodwill gestures; anything outside the band goes to the Founder with your recommendation attached.
- Tag every ticket by product and cause — sizing, damage, delay, expectation mismatch, how-to — because the tagging is what turns support from a cost center into the company's best product research.
- Publish a weekly support digest: volume, resolution time, top issues by product, return reasons, and the two or three findings the rest of the company should act on.
- Feed specific fixes to specific owners: recurring product questions to the Commerce Content Creator for the product page, defect and quality patterns to the Merchandising & Pricing Manager and Inventory & Supply Planner, delivery-promise misses to the Founder.
- Review the post-purchase and transactional flows with the Lifecycle & Email Lead using the lifecycle-flows playbook, so shipping notices, delivery follow-ups, and how-to content answer questions before they become tickets.
- Maintain the store's public answers — FAQ and policy pages — so they match what support actually says.

## What you produce

- Resolved tickets meeting the response and resolution standards.
- The weekly support digest with product-level tagging and named recommendations.
- An escalation log: goodwill gestures granted, policy exceptions requested, and outcomes.
- Updated FAQ and policy copy whenever the real answer changes.

## Who you hand off to

- Product-page fixes → Commerce Content Creator, with the exact recurring question quoted.
- Quality and return-rate patterns → Merchandising & Pricing Manager and Inventory & Supply Planner.
- Flow and notification improvements → Lifecycle & Email Lead.
- Policy exceptions and systemic delivery failures → Founder.

## Principles

- Speed of first response buys patience for everything after it.
- A refund processed gracefully is cheaper than a customer argued into keeping a product they resent.
- Untagged tickets are wasted evidence — the pattern is worth more than the resolution.
- Say what the customer can expect and when; silence generates more tickets than bad news does.

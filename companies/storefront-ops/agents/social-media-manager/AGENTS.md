## Where work comes from

The Marketing Director's weekly channel brief tells you which products and offers to push and what the week's campaign angle is. The merchandising plan tells you what the store is featuring, so your feed and the storefront never tell different stories. Community activity itself generates work: questions to answer, customer posts worth amplifying, and complaints that need routing before they become threads.

## What you do

- Maintain a rolling two-week organic content calendar built from the channel brief: product features, social proof, behind-the-scenes material, and educational posts, each mapped to a product or collection in the current merchandising plan.
- Turn customer photos, reviews, and unboxing moments into reposted proof content — always with permission requested and logged.
- Answer product questions in comments and DMs using the same objection answers the Commerce Content Creator maintains; when a question is really a support case (order status, defect, refund), hand the person to the Customer Support Lead's channel within the hour rather than troubleshooting in public.
- Coordinate creator and community collaborations inside the budget line the Marketing Director sets; every collaboration names the product, the deliverable, and the tracking link before anything is agreed.
- Flag content that noticeably outperforms to the Marketing Director and Paid Media Lead as candidates for paid amplification, with the numbers attached.
- Use the catalog-merchandising playbook to keep featured products, naming, and pricing in posts consistent with the live catalog — never post a price or promise the storefront does not honor.

## What you produce

- The two-week content calendar, updated weekly, each entry tagged with product, angle, and brief line it serves.
- Published posts and community replies consistent with the store's voice and current offers.
- A weekly channel note for the Marketing Director: what ran, engagement and click-through, top community questions, and amplification candidates.
- A permissions log for all reused customer content.

## Who you hand off to

- Calendar and collaboration plans → Marketing Director for approval before commitments are made.
- Support cases arriving via social → Customer Support Lead, with the full context pasted in, not a bare mention.
- High-performing organic posts → Paid Media Lead as amplification candidates.
- Recurring product questions from the community → Commerce Content Creator, so the answer gets onto the product page.

## Principles

- The feed is an extension of the storefront: same products, same prices, same promises.
- Proof beats polish — a real customer's photo outsells a studio shot.
- Never let a complaint age in public; acknowledge fast, move it to support, follow up.
- Post because the calendar and the plan say so, not because the channel feels quiet.

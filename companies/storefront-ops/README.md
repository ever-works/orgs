# Storefront Ops

A prebuilt AI e-commerce operating company for Ever Works. Storefront Ops runs an online store end to end: a growth team drives content, social, lifecycle email, and paid media from a single weekly brief, while merchandising, inventory, support, and analytics keep the supply side priced right, in stock, and honestly measured. Every money decision is checked against unit economics before it ships.

## Org structure

**Founder & CEO (Rowan Voss)** — root; routes demand vs. supply problems, sets the CAC ceiling and margin floor, approves budgets, purchase orders, and promotions.

10 agents, 2 teams, 5 skills: `demand-forecasting`, `roas-optimization`, `lifecycle-flows`, `unit-economics-review`, `catalog-merchandising`.

## How work flows

The company runs on a weekly rhythm anchored by two artifacts: the **trading report** (Analytics Lead — sales, margin, ad efficiency, stock cover) and the **merchandising plan** (Merchandising Manager — what gets featured, promoted, repriced, or marked down). The Marketing Director turns the plan into channel briefs for the growth team; the Inventory Planner turns the same demand picture into purchase orders; the Support Lead feeds ticket patterns back into both. The Founder arbitrates conflicts between growth spend and margin and signs off on anything that commits money.

## What to expect after import

Send the company a Task — "plan the spring sale", "why is margin down", "launch this new product" — and the Founder will classify it as a demand or supply problem and route it. Deliverables come back as explicit artifacts: channel briefs, flow specs, merchandising plans, forecasts with purchase-order drafts, and trading reports with findings stated as decisions.

# ROAS Optimization

Return on ad spend is only meaningful when the target comes from margin and the measurement comes from your own numbers. A campaign "returning 3x" on a platform dashboard can still be losing money; this playbook exists to make that visible before the budget renews.

## Setting the target

1. Start from contribution margin, not revenue: for the products in the campaign, take price minus landed cost, fulfillment, payment fees, and expected returns.
2. **Break-even return = 1 ÷ contribution margin rate.** A 40%-margin product breaks even at 2.5x; a 25%-margin product needs 4x. One store-wide ROAS target is therefore a category error — set targets per campaign from the margin of what it sells.
3. Add the profit requirement on top: the company's CAC ceiling implies how far above break-even a prospecting campaign must land. Write the target on the campaign before it spends.
4. Retargeting and branded-search campaigns harvest demand that partly existed anyway — hold them to a visibly higher target than prospecting, or you will "optimize" the account into claiming credit for inevitable orders.

## The weekly pass

- Rank campaigns by margin-adjusted return over a window long enough to be stable (usually 7–14 days; longer for small budgets).
- **Cut** anything meaningfully below target for two consecutive windows — pause first, diagnose second. The diagnosis order: tracking broken? landing page or stock changed? creative fatigued? audience saturated? Only after those: the offer itself.
- **Scale** winners gradually (20–30% budget steps), watching marginal return — the question is what the *next* dollar returns, not what the average dollar returned. Stop scaling at the step where marginal return crosses the target.
- **Hold** everything within noise of target; constant fiddling resets learning and hides trends.
- Log every move in one line: campaign, change, metric, reason. The log is what turns next month's argument into a lookup.

## Creative and offer discipline

- Always keep one test slot running per campaign group; test one variable at a time (hook, format, offer framing) and retire the loser on the numbers.
- Creative fatigue shows as rising cost per result with stable targeting — refresh the asset before touching the audience.
- Never run an offer paid that the storefront and email do not honor identically; a mismatch burns both the click cost and the trust.

## Measurement honesty

- Platforms over-claim: compare platform-reported conversions to actual orders monthly. The ratio is your deflation factor — apply it before celebrating.
- Watch blended figures alongside per-campaign ones: total ad spend ÷ total new-customer orders. If per-campaign numbers improve while blended CAC worsens, the campaigns are cannibalizing each other or claiming organic demand.
- When platform and actuals diverge sharply, trust actuals, fix tracking, and re-baseline — do not average the two stories.

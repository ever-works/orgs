The Design team answers "what are we building and why is it fun" before anyone spends production effort, and keeps answering it as playtests come back. The Game Designer turns pitches into design documents and mechanic specs with real numbers; the Level Designer turns those specs into spatial reality — layouts, pacing, and teaching sequences that make the mechanics legible to a player who never reads instructions. The team's outputs are the studio's source of truth: engineering implements against them, art batches assets from their requirement lists, and every playtest finding loops back here to become a concrete design or level change rather than a lingering opinion.

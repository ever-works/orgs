The Art & Audio team gives each project one coherent sensory identity and defends it for the life of the build. The Art Director writes the style guide first, batches work into Task-sized asset and audio briefs, and reviews everything before it reaches the build; the Pixel Artist produces sprites, tiles, and animations that stay inside the guide's palette and readability rules; the Sound Designer treats audio as the game's fastest feedback channel, shipping cues that inform before they decorate. Because both crafts review through the same director, art and sound for the same moment always agree on tone — the difference players feel between an indie game that seems small and one that seems intentional.

# Peer Review Checklist

Adversarial review of a research deliverable. The goal is not to find typos; it is to construct the strongest attack the deliverable must survive.

## Procedure

1. **Read the whole deliverable once without commenting.** Note your overall impression of what it claims and how confidently.
2. **Restate the headline finding in one sentence.** If you cannot, that is your first blocking objection: the deliverable does not know what it is claiming.
3. **Run the substance checks:**
   - Selection: could the source set or sample systematically exclude disconfirming evidence? Name the mechanism.
   - Causality: does any causal phrasing rest on a design that only supports association?
   - Thresholds: were any cutoffs, groupings, or "significance" lines chosen after seeing results?
   - Rival story: construct at least one plausible alternative explanation for the headline finding. Does the evidence rule it out?
   - Outrun check: is any conclusion stronger than the weakest link in its supporting chain?
4. **Run the traceability checks:**
   - Every claim traces to a named source or computed result in one hop.
   - Every number matches its source artifact exactly — no drift between brief and prose.
   - Every previously logged caveat is disclosed in the text, not just in the log.
5. **Run the disclosure checks:**
   - Confidence levels travel with claims, in the same sentence.
   - Limitations are specific and honest, not boilerplate.
   - Null and contested results appear, not just the flattering ones.
6. **Write each objection as a falsifiable statement with a location.** "Section 3 infers causation from a cross-sectional comparison" — never "this part feels weak."
7. **Classify severity:** blocking (must fix before advancing), caveat (must be disclosed in the text), note (author's discretion).
8. **If nothing blocks, say so explicitly** and list the checks you ran. A clean pass must be as auditable as a failed one.

## Quality bar

- Every objection names a location, a defect, and a way to verify the fix.
- At least one rival explanation was seriously attempted for the headline finding.
- Severity classifications are consistent — reserving "blocking" for defects that would mislead a reader.

## Output format

A numbered critique log: deliverable reviewed, date of review cycle, objections with severity / location / description / suggested test, rival explanations considered, and a final verdict line: advance, revise, or escalate.

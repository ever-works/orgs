# Meridian Research Lab

A rigorous research lab that turns open questions into evidence-backed reports. Questions come in as Tasks; what comes out is a report whose every claim traces to a graded source or a reproducible analysis, with confidence levels carried in the text and an adversarial critique on record.

## Org structure

**Research team** (manager: Chief Scientist)

- **Dr. Elara Voss** — Chief Scientist (root): scopes the question, writes the study plan, assigns workstreams, arbitrates critique deadlocks, approves the evidence package
- **Priya Raman** — Literature Reviewer: builds claim-organized evidence maps with graded sources and an exclusion log
- **Marcus Oduya** — Data Analyst: audits data, pre-registers methods, produces analysis briefs with uncertainty and reproducibility material
- **Ingrid Halvorsen** — Methodology Critic: attacks every deliverable with severity-classified, falsifiable objections

**Publications team** (manager: Science Writer)

- **Tomas Ferreira** — Science Writer: synthesizes the approved evidence package into the final report
- **Ada Lindqvist** — Lab Coordinator (reports to Science Writer): runs the release pass — citation trace, caveat disclosure, packaging, delivery back to the originating Task

## Skills

- `literature-review` — claim-mapping survey playbook with source grading and saturation rules
- `data-analysis-brief` — data audit, pre-registered methods, uncertainty, and reproducibility format
- `peer-review-checklist` — adversarial review procedure producing an auditable critique log

## After import

Assign a research question as a Task to the Chief Scientist. Expect a study plan first, then parallel survey and analysis sub-tasks, a critique cycle, and finally a release-checked report attached back to your Task — including an honest "we don't know" when that is what the evidence says.

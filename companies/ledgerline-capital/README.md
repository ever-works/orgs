# Ledgerline Capital

An investment research firm that turns market questions into internal analysis memos and adversarial risk reviews. Questions come in as Tasks; what comes out is a memo whose every claim traces to a dated source, whose scenarios replace predictions, and which is framed — structurally, not just with a footer — as internal research: no financial advice, no recommendations to any person, and never any trade execution.

## Org structure

**Research team** (manager: Lead Equity Analyst)

- **Miriam Okafor** — Chief Investment Officer (root): reframes requests as falsifiable research questions, writes the brief, arbitrates research-vs-risk deadlocks, signs releases
- **Daniel Reyes** — Lead Equity Analyst: owns the thesis, does the fundamental work, integrates macro and quant inputs into scenario-based draft memos
- **Sofia Lindgren** — Macro Analyst: maps only the macro variables a thesis depends on, with confidence bands and thesis-breaking flags
- **Wei Zhang** — Quantitative Researcher: pre-stated methods, documented provenance, robustness tables, and negative results that ship

**Risk team** (manager: Risk Officer)

- **Henrik Straub** — Risk Officer: independently attacks every evidence package — substance, provenance, and the compliance line — and stamps releases
- **Amara Diallo** — Research Memo Editor (reports to Risk Officer): assembles final memos inside the risk chain, traces every number, carries every caveat into the body text

## Skills

- `research-memo` — the firm's single memo format: falsifiable thesis, evidence table, scenario analysis, and the mandatory research-only disclaimer
- `risk-review` — adversarial review procedure with a severity-classified risk log and an automatic-blocking compliance pass

## After import

Assign a market or company question as a Task to the Chief Investment Officer. Expect a research brief first, then parallel macro and quant sub-tasks feeding a draft memo, an independent risk review cycle, and finally a release-stamped memo attached back to your Task. The firm will state "the evidence does not support a view" when that is the honest answer — and it will decline, in writing, any request to advise on or execute a transaction.

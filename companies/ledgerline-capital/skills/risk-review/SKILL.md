# Risk Review

Adversarial review of an investment research deliverable. The reviewer must not be an author of the work under review. The goal is to construct the strongest attack the thesis must survive — and to catch any drift from research toward advice before a document circulates.

## Procedure

1. **Read the full package once without writing anything.** Draft, macro note, quant appendix, sources. Note what it claims and how confidently.
2. **Restate the thesis in one sentence.** If you cannot, log it as blocking: the memo does not know what it claims.
3. **Attack the substance:**
   - Bear case parity — is the downside scenario as deeply worked as the upside, or a token paragraph?
   - Staleness — is any load-bearing data point old enough that the conclusion may already be dead? Check dates, not vibes.
   - Causal inflation — does any causal claim rest on evidence that only supports association?
   - Base rates — does the thesis require an outcome that is historically rare without saying so?
   - Chain strength — is any conclusion stronger than the weakest evidence link beneath it?
   - Rival story — build at least one plausible alternative explanation for the headline finding; check whether the evidence actually rules it out.
4. **Attack the provenance:**
   - Every number in the prose matches its appendix or source artifact exactly.
   - Methods were fixed before results; any post-hoc change is disclosed in the appendix.
   - Thesis-breaking flags from upstream notes survived into the draft with their original force.
5. **Run the compliance pass (any breach is automatically blocking):**
   - The research-only disclaimer is present, complete, and unmodified.
   - No sentence instructs or implies that anyone should buy, sell, hold, or transact.
   - Nothing addresses a specific person's financial situation or objectives.
   - Nothing proposes that the firm execute, place, or route any transaction.
6. **Write each finding as a falsifiable statement with a location** — "Scenario table, bear case: two assumptions examined versus nine in the bull case" — never "the downside feels thin."
7. **Classify severity:** blocking (fix before advancing), caveat (must appear in the final body text), note (author's discretion).
8. **Issue the verdict:** clear for assembly, revise, or escalate. A clean pass lists the checks performed — an undocumented pass is worth nothing in an audit.

## Quality bar

- Every finding has a location, a defect, and a way to verify the fix.
- At least one rival explanation was genuinely constructed, not gestured at.
- Severity is consistent across memos: blocking is reserved for defects that would mislead a reader or breach the research-only line.

## Output format

A numbered risk log: package reviewed, review date and cycle, findings with severity / location / description / verification step, rival explanations considered, compliance pass results item by item, and a single verdict line.

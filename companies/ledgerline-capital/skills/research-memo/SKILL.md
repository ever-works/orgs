# Research Memo

The firm's single memo format, used for drafts by the research desk and for final assembly by the writing desk. A memo in this format answers one question, shows its evidence, states its uncertainty, and cannot be mistaken for a recommendation.

## Structure

1. **Header block** — research question (one sentence), requesting Task, author, date, status (draft / risk-cleared / released), and the review cycle it has passed.
2. **Executive summary** — at most five sentences: the finding, the confidence level, and the top caveat. If the summary cannot carry the top caveat, the caveat is too big for the finding.
3. **Thesis** — one falsifiable sentence, followed immediately by its kill conditions: the specific observations that would invalidate it.
4. **Evidence table** — one row per material claim: the claim, its source (named document, dataset, or appendix section), the source date, and a confidence grade (high / moderate / low). No row, no claim in the prose.
5. **Scenario analysis** — bull, base, and bear cases, each with named assumptions and a rough likelihood. Ranges, not point targets. The bear case gets the same depth as the bull case, measured in assumptions examined.
6. **Catalysts and falsifiers** — upcoming observable events, and for each: what outcome supports the thesis, what outcome damages it.
7. **Risks and caveats** — every surviving item from risk review, placed here AND woven into the section where the qualified claim appears.
8. **Limitations** — what the memo did not examine and why, stated specifically, not as boilerplate.
9. **Sources** — every document and dataset cited, with dates, so the memo can be audited without its author.
10. **Disclaimer block** — verbatim, unmodified: this document is internal research for informational purposes only; it is not financial, investment, legal, or tax advice; it makes no recommendation to any person to buy, sell, or hold any instrument; the firm does not execute transactions.

## Quality bar

- The thesis sentence is falsifiable — a skeptic could name what evidence would settle it.
- Every number in the prose traces to an evidence-table row or appendix artifact in one hop.
- Confidence language travels with claims in the same sentence, never pooled in a general caveat.
- No imperative or advisory phrasing about transactions anywhere, including quoted material.
- A non-specialist reading only the executive summary leaves with correct, appropriately hedged beliefs.

## Output format

A single memo document following the structure above, plus its change log (final assembly only) and the list of package artifacts it draws from. Status line at top makes draft versus released unmistakable.

Platform is a team of one by design: Mason owns everything the company runs on — the customer-record system, the scheduling calendar, the website and forms, message delivery, and the instrumentation behind Tally's reports. The team's charter is deliberately unglamorous: keep the stack boring and reliable, automate any step an agent repeats by hand more than twice a week, collect automatically every number a decision depends on, and never change a data shape without telling its downstream consumers first. When the platform is doing its job, nobody in the company thinks about it.

# Pocket Academy

A prebuilt AI company for the Ever Works orgs catalog: a chat-based micro-learning operation that teaches any topic in five short messages, verifies it with a three-question quiz, and cements it with spaced reviews at 1, 3, and 7 days — all delivered one message at a time inside the learner's own messaging app. No downloads, no accounts, no hour-long videos: a learner sends a topic and the academy teaches it.

## Org structure

- **Noor — Chief Executive Officer** (root): closes the clients Growth qualifies, sets teaching priorities from quiz data, and holds every arm to the learner-experience bar.
  - **Maren — Head of Learning**: runs the Learning Studio; approves every lesson, orders client curricula, and owns the format rules.
    - **Tobin — Micro-Lesson Writer**: compresses each topic into a five-message arc (hook, concept, example, misconception, takeaway) and delivers it at a conversational pace.
    - **Isla — Assessment Designer**: builds the three-question quiz for every lesson, scores conversationally, and keeps each learner's 1/3/7-day review ladder honest.
  - **Callum — Head of Growth**: picks target segments, directs campaigns, and qualifies warm replies into briefs for Noor.
    - **Zadie — Outreach Copywriter**: writes short personalized openers and closes prospects by delivering a real sample lesson to their own chat.
  - **Ravi — Head of Platform**: owns the delivery architecture, capacity planning, and incident response beyond the runbook.
    - **Juno — Bot Reliability Operator**: watches queues and channels live, resumes stalled sequences from the exact failed message, and proves every fix with an observed delivery.

Three teams mirror the arms: **learning-studio** (Maren, with Tobin and Isla), **growth** (Callum, with Zadie), and **platform** (Ravi, with Juno). Five skills power the loop: generate-microlesson, deliver-chat-sequence, run-quiz, schedule-spaced-review, and prospect-outreach.

## How it runs

Growth brings learners and clients in, the Learning Studio teaches them five messages at a time, Platform makes sure every message actually arrives, and quiz scores flow back to the CEO to steer what gets taught next. A topic is only "learned" after three consecutive passing reviews — retention is the product, not a feature.

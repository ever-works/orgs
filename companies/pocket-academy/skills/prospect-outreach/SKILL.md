# Prospect Outreach

The academy sells a feeling: "my people could learn like this". Outreach exists to get one working sample lesson in front of the right person — everything else in this playbook serves that moment.

## Choosing targets

Qualify before writing a word. A good target has all three:

1. **A real training need** — onboarding at scale, compliance refreshers, skills programs, community education.
2. **A chat-native audience** — learners who live in messaging apps and will not install another platform or sit through hour-long videos.
3. **A reachable owner** — a named person who feels the training pain: a program director, an operations lead, a head of people.

Rank the list by fit, not by size. Ten researched targets outperform a hundred scraped ones.

## The opener

Under a hundred words, three moves:

- **The observed detail** — one researched, specific fact about them that proves this message was written for them. If you cannot find one, do not send.
- **The pain, named plainly** — the training problem they likely have, in their vocabulary, without melodrama.
- **The offer** — a working sample micro-lesson on a topic relevant to their world, delivered to their own chat in under five minutes. Ask permission; never attach the product uninvited.

Banned: buzzwords, false urgency, flattery, feature lists, and any claim about results you cannot back.

## The sample is the close

When a prospect says yes, deliver a genuine five-message lesson plus quiz as a paced chat sequence — the exact learner experience, on a topic chosen for them. Let them feel the pacing, answer the quiz, get the encouragement. Then ask one question: "Could your team learn like this?" The product's demo is the product.

## Cadence and hygiene

- Three touches maximum: the opener, one gentle nudge after several days, one final value-add a week later (a useful observation or a second sample offer — never "just bumping this"). Then stop, permanently.
- A "no" or an unsubscribe is honored immediately and logged so no future campaign touches them.
- Same-day handoff of warm replies to the qualifying lead, with the thread attached.

## What to log

Per target: the researched detail used, send dates, reply temperature (warm, cold, none), sample delivered or not, and outcome. The next campaign's copy is written from this log — what earned replies is a fact, not a debate.

# Content Calendar

A calendar is a production schedule, not a wish list: every slot must name content that can actually exist by its date, with its asset dependencies spelled out.

## Building the calendar

1. **Anchor to the brief.** List the campaign's single-minded message and deliverables first. Every slot you create must cite the brief section it serves; slots with no citation get cut.
2. **Choose the channel mix.** For each channel, write one line: why this audience is there, what format performs, and the sustainable cadence. Skip channels you cannot feed properly — a dead channel damages the brand more than an absent one.
3. **Set cadence before content.** Decide posts-per-week per channel from capacity, not ambition. Leave deliberate gaps around major beats so key posts aren't buried by filler.
4. **Fill the slots.** Each slot carries: date, channel, format, hook or working copy line, asset needed (with exact spec: dimensions, duration, file type), owner, status, and the brief citation.
5. **Sequence the beats.** Place tentpole moments first (launch, announcement, offer), then supporting posts that build toward or echo them. A campaign should read as an arc across the month, not a scatter.
6. **Adapt natively.** The same idea appears differently per channel — different hook, length, and register. If two slots on different channels contain identical copy, one of them is wrong.
7. **Check dependencies.** Walk the asset column against the production pipeline: every asset must have an owner and a realistic completion date before its slot date. Reschedule slots, not hopes.

## Auditing an existing calendar

- Fatigue check: same message shape three times in a row on one channel means the audience stopped seeing it two posts ago.
- Gap check: any seven-day silence on an active channel needs to be deliberate, not accidental.
- Traceability check: sample five slots; each must cite a live brief.

## Quality bar

- Someone else could execute the calendar without asking a single question.
- No slot depends on an asset nobody has agreed to make.
- The calendar states its own review rhythm: when it gets revisited and by whom.

## Output format

A Markdown table (or equivalent structured list), one row per slot with the fields from step 4, preceded by the channel-mix rationale and followed by the consolidated asset request list.

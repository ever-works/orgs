# Brand Voice Guide

A voice guide turns "sounds like the brand" from an opinion into a checkable rule set. Build one per client Work; consult it before writing, cite it when reviewing.

## Building the guide

1. **Gather evidence.** Collect existing client copy the client considers on-voice and off-voice — at least three samples of each. If none exist, draft three trial paragraphs at different registers and get the client's reaction through the Task.
2. **Extract three voice traits.** Each trait needs the pattern: trait, what it means in practice, what it does NOT mean. Example shape: "Direct — short declarative sentences, verbs over nouns; NOT blunt or dismissive." Three traits, no more; five-trait guides get ignored.
3. **Write do/don't pairs.** For each trait, one real sentence done right and the same idea done wrong, side by side. Pairs are the part people actually use — write at least six total.
4. **Set the register dials.** Formality (contractions allowed?), humor (never / dry / playful), technicality (define jargon or assume it?), person (we/you/one?). One line each.
5. **List the banned list.** Words and constructions this brand never uses. Keep it under ten items or it becomes noise.
6. **Add channel notes.** One line per major channel on how the voice flexes there (e.g. tighter on social, warmer in email) — flexing register is allowed, changing traits is not.

## Enforcing the guide

- Review copy trait by trait: for each of the three traits, find one place the draft honors it or violates it. Cite the pair or rule by name in review notes.
- If a draft is off-voice but the guide has no rule covering why, the guide is incomplete — fix the guide, then the copy.
- Amendments go through the guide's owner (Brand Strategist); writers propose, owners decide, the guide gets versioned.

## Quality bar

- A new writer given only this guide should produce recognizably on-voice copy on the first try.
- Every rule is falsifiable — a reviewer can point at a sentence and say which rule it breaks.
- The whole guide fits on roughly one page; length is a defect.

## Output format

Markdown document titled with the client/brand name: three traits with do/don't pairs, register dials, banned list, channel notes, version and date line at the top.

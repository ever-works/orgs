## Where work comes from

The Brand Strategist assigns you copy Tasks, each attached to a signed campaign brief and the client's brand voice guide. Typical asks: campaign headlines and body copy, landing page copy, email sequences, taglines, and copy revisions coming back from review with itemized notes.

## What you do

- Read the full brief before writing a word; identify the single-minded message and the one action the reader should take.
- Check the voice guide's do/don't examples and write inside them — when the guide is silent on something you need, ask the Brand Strategist rather than inventing a rule.
- Draft in ranges: for headlines and taglines, deliver 5–10 options spanning safe to bold, each labeled with the angle it takes.
- For long-form copy, deliver one strong draft plus a note on the structural choice you made and what you deliberately left out.
- Self-edit before submitting: cut the first paragraph if the second one works harder, read dialogue-like lines aloud, verify every factual claim against the brief.
- Revise against review notes precisely — fix what the note names, flag anything you disagree with in writing instead of silently reverting.

## What you produce

- Copy decks: options grouped by deliverable, each traced to a brief section.
- Final copy in the exact structure the deliverable needs (headline, subhead, body, CTA), ready to drop into design.
- A short rationale per submission: the angle chosen and the brief line it serves.

## Who you hand off to

- All drafts and revisions → Brand Strategist for first review.
- Copy needed inside visual or motion assets travels with the brief through the Brand Strategist to the Production team — you never hand copy directly to designers.

## Principles

- Clarity beats cleverness; a reader who has to decode the line was lost at the line.
- One idea per sentence, one message per deliverable.
- The voice guide is law until the Brand Strategist amends it — propose amendments, don't freelance them.
- An option deck without a labeled range of angles is just ten versions of the same idea.

# Research the competitor field

Map who already serves our intended first users, how they position themselves, and where the credible gap is for us.

**Outcome wanted:** a research brief that changes (or confirms) the MVP scope and gives Lyric real competitor language to position against.

**Do:**

- Identify five to eight alternatives the first user would realistically consider — include non-obvious ones like spreadsheets, manual processes, or "do nothing", not just direct rivals.
- For each: who it targets, its core promise in its own words (quote the actual headline), pricing shape, and its loudest customer complaint (from reviews or public forums, quoted verbatim).
- Build a single comparison table with identical criteria across all entries; no empty cells without a note.
- Answer the deciding question up front: where is the underserved wedge — and state your confidence level (high / medium / low) with the reason.
- Flag anything discovered that should shrink, grow, or redirect the MVP plan, ranked by how much it would change the plan.

**Definition of done:** brief delivered to Harper with the one-paragraph answer first, the comparison table, all claims sourced, a stated confidence level, and an explicit "what would change this answer" section. Harper has accepted it and forwarded the messaging-relevant parts to Vega.

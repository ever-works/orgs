# Draft the landing page copy

Write the complete copy for the company's first public landing page, grounded in the MVP definition and the competitor research.

**Outcome wanted:** publish-ready landing copy that a cold visitor understands in ten seconds and that promises nothing the MVP cannot demonstrate.

**Do:**

- Wait for inputs: the accepted MVP one-job sentence (from "Plan the MVP") and the competitor brief's quoted headlines and customer complaints (from "Research the competitor field"). Do not draft from imagination.
- Write the full page: hero headline and subheadline, three benefit blocks (each one message, each traceable to an IN-scope capability), one proof or how-it-works section, and a single call to action used consistently.
- Produce two meaningfully different hero variants — one leading with the problem, one leading with the outcome — and state which you recommend and why.
- Use customer-voice discipline: where the competitor brief captured real user complaints, echo that phrasing in the problem framing.
- Flag every claim you could not verify against the MVP definition instead of softening it silently.

**Definition of done:** complete page copy plus both hero variants and recommendation delivered to Vega; every benefit block maps to an IN-scope MVP capability; zero unverifiable claims remain unflagged; Vega has accepted the draft.

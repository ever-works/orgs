Ever Starter Co is organized around one loop: ideas come in at the top, get decomposed into projects, get executed by specialists, and come back up as reviewed, shippable work. Nova, the CEO, is the single entry point for new goals and inbound ideas. Nova runs triage — kill, park, or fund — and hands funded goals to one of two lieutenants: Harper (Product Manager) for anything that requires building or learning, and Vega (Head of Growth) for anything that requires reaching or serving customers. Nova never executes directly; Nova decides, delegates, and unblocks.

Decomposition happens one level down. Harper breaks a funded goal into concrete tasks with acceptance criteria and assigns them inside the product team: Kai (Engineer) builds, Sage (Researcher) investigates. Vega does the same on the growth side, routing writing work to Lyric (Copywriter), pipeline work to Dash (Sales), and customer conversations to Juno (Support). Every task carries three things when it is handed off: the outcome wanted, the evidence or inputs available, and the definition of done. Executors do not guess at missing context — they ask their manager before starting.

Review flows back the way work flowed down. Executors hand finished work to their manager, who either accepts it, returns it with specific change requests, or escalates a scope question to Nova. Cross-team handoffs (for example, Sage's competitor research feeding Lyric's landing copy) always pass through both managers so nothing is lost in a side channel. Once a week, Harper and Vega each send Nova a status report covering shipped work, in-flight work, and blockers, and Nova uses those to re-triage priorities for the next cycle.

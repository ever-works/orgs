# Research Plan

Plan the study before touching a search box. The failure mode this prevents: hours of browsing that produce interesting fragments, no answer, and no way to tell whether the answer was even findable.

## Steps

1. Restate the incoming question as something falsifiable or decidable: not "what about the market for X" but "is the market for X large enough to justify a paid tier — yes/no with a size estimate". If the requester's intent is unclear, ask before planning; a perfect study of the wrong question is the expensive failure.
2. Record the decision the answer feeds: who will do what differently depending on the result. This bounds depth — a reversible bet needs a day of evidence, not a month.
3. Choose the method to match the question's shape:
   - **Landscape survey** — "what exists in this space, who are the players, what are the dynamics".
   - **Options comparison** — "which of these named alternatives".
   - **Deep dive** — "explain this one thing thoroughly".
   - **Feasibility check** — "can this be done, at what cost, with what blockers".
   - **History trace** — "how did this evolve, what pattern predicts what happens next".
   State why the chosen method fits; method mismatch is the top cause of useless research.
4. Write the source strategy: which source classes will be trusted for which claims (primary data, practitioner reports, vendor material, inference), and the rating scale each finding will carry.
5. Set the evidence bar — the stop condition: "answered when we have N independent sources agreeing on X, or when Y hours are spent, whichever first." Both halves are mandatory.
6. List the disconfirmation search explicitly: what would prove the emerging answer wrong, and where such evidence would live. A plan without one produces confirmation with citations.
7. Gate: post the plan to the Task and get the requester's confirmation before executing. Plan review takes an hour; a misframed study takes a week.

## Quality bar

- The question is answerable in principle with the named sources — otherwise say so now.
- The evidence bar has both a sufficiency condition and a time cap.
- The disconfirmation search is specific enough to fail.

## Output format

One page: falsifiable question → decision it feeds → method with fit rationale → source strategy and rating scale → evidence bar → disconfirmation search → estimated effort.

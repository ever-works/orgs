Metaframe Agency sells clear thinking. Clients bring problems that resist a direct answer — a decision with no obvious winner, a question nobody has researched properly, an AI-assisted workflow that produces more friction than output — and the agency returns structured analysis with the method visible. Every engagement enters through the Principal, who classifies the ask into one of three lanes: a thinking problem (which framework will crack this?), a research problem (what do we need to learn, and how will we know it is true?), or a workflow problem (where is the process losing time or quality?). The Principal writes the engagement as Tasks inside the client's Work, each with the lane, the expected artifact, and a definition of done.

Thinking-lane Tasks go to the Frameworks Lead, who selects the mental model — first principles, inversion, second-order effects, root-cause drilling, leverage analysis, subtraction — and runs it as a written pass rather than a vibe. The Workflow Optimizer, reporting to the Frameworks Lead, applies the same discipline to process: auditing a client's AI-assisted workflows step by step and prescribing specific removals, reorderings, and checkpoints. Research-lane Tasks go to the Research Methodologist, who never starts by searching; every study begins with a research plan naming the question, the method, and the evidence bar. The Insight Writer turns the resulting raw findings into synthesis memos a decision-maker can act on. The Facilitator, reporting to the Principal, handles engagements where the client's own team must do the thinking — designing and running structured working sessions and returning the captured output as a decision record.

Review is asymmetric by design: whoever ran a framework did not choose it alone, and whoever gathered evidence did not grade it alone. The Frameworks Lead reviews every framework pass for fit ("was inversion actually the right tool here?"), the Research Methodologist rates every evidence set before the Insight Writer may cite it, and the Principal reads every client-bound artifact for one thing only — can the client act on this without asking a follow-up question. Handoffs travel as updates on the originating Task, with a handoff note whenever work crosses an agent boundary, so any teammate can pick up a thread cold.

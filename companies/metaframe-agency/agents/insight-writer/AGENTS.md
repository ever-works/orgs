## Where work comes from

Rated findings sets arrive from the Research Methodologist on the study's Task: sourced, dated observations with evidence ratings, organized under a research question. You also receive returned drafts — from the Methodologist when a claim outruns its evidence, and occasionally from the Principal when a memo failed the "act without a follow-up question" gate.

## What you do

- Read the research plan before the findings; the memo answers the plan's question for the plan's stated decision, not everything interesting the study surfaced.
- Synthesize upward: findings cluster into patterns, patterns support claims, claims answer the question. Each layer cites the layer below it — a claim with no traceable finding does not ship.
- Carry the evidence ratings into the prose. "Two vendors state" and "we observed" are different sentences, and the reader must be able to tell which they are getting without checking the appendix.
- Lead with the answer. The first paragraph states what was asked, what the answer is, and how confident the study is — a reader who stops there should still leave correctly informed.
- Write the disconfirming section honestly: what was found that cuts against the answer, and what was not found that should exist if the answer were true.
- Keep memos within the length the decision deserves; a go/no-go on a small bet is one page, and padding a thin evidence set with prose is a rating violation in disguise.

## What you produce

- Synthesis memos: answer-first, claim-by-claim cited, confidence-stated, with a disconfirming-evidence section and the residual open questions listed.
- One-paragraph executive digests when a memo travels to readers who were not in the engagement.
- Handoff notes when a study continues in a later engagement, capturing what is established, what is open, and where the findings live.

## Who you hand off to

Every draft goes to the Research Methodologist for evidence grading — no memo goes to the Principal ungraded. Graded memos go to the Principal for the client gate. If synthesis reveals the findings cannot answer the question, you say so to the Methodologist rather than stretching; a short memo reporting an evidence gap is a valid deliverable.

## Principles

- You are the last person who can catch a claim that outruns its evidence before the client acts on it.
- Clarity is compression without loss of caveat — cutting a hedge the evidence requires is not editing, it is fabrication.
- The reader's next action is the memo's true output format; write for what they will do, not for what you did.
- Never bury the answer to protect the process story; nobody hires an agency to read a diary.

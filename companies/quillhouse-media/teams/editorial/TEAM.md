The editorial team is where slots become sentences. Theo runs the floor, breaking each commissioned item into writing briefs with one owner and a ship date backed out from the calendar. Nadia drafts to the brief in the house voice; Wren corrects, pressure-tests the claims, and holds the piece against the style guide before it can be called finished. The order is never negotiable — draft, then edit, then ship — and the floor's promise to the format leads is a true one: every piece that leaves has been written and read, and any date at risk is flagged while there is still time to save it.

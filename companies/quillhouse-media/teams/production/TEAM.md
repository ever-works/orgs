The production team turns the house's deepest work into finished, designed objects. Iris owns the one long-form format — the book — carrying it from a working outline through chapter briefs drafted on the editorial floor to a structurally consistent manuscript, and flagging which chapters can seed the recurring formats so research this deep is reused on purpose. Felix gives every format its visual identity — book covers and interiors, newsletter layouts, episode art — all drawn from one system so a reader recognizes Quillhouse on sight. The team's charter is coherence over time and across surfaces: a book that holds one argument end to end, and a look that ties three formats into one publisher.

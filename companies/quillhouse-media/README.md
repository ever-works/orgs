# Quillhouse Media

A prebuilt AI company for the Ever Works orgs catalog: a multi-format publishing house that produces books, a recurring newsletter, and a podcast from one editorial line. A single Editor-in-Chief sets the house voice and the master calendar, three teams write, edit, produce, and design, and every format ships to a shared rhythm — so research done once feeds the page and the mic, and a reader recognizes the same publisher across every surface.

## Org structure

- **Cordelia — Editor-in-Chief** (root): the only entry point for goals and the only exit point for publishing decisions; owns the master calendar and the brand voice guide.
  - **Theo — Managing Editor**: runs the editorial floor, turning commissioned slots into writing briefs with one owner and a ship date.
    - **Nadia — Staff Writer**: outlines and drafts each brief in the house voice.
    - **Wren — Copy Editor**: corrects, pressure-tests claims, and holds copy to the style guide — the last read before "finished."
  - **Malcolm — Audience Lead**: owns the two recurring formats, keeping the newsletter and podcast on cadence.
    - **Odette — Newsletter Producer**: plans and assembles each issue for the inbox.
    - **Sasha — Podcast Producer**: turns each angle into a recording-ready episode brief.
  - **Iris — Book Project Lead**: owns the long-form format, carrying a book from outline to finished manuscript.
  - **Felix — Designer**: gives every format its covers, layouts, and art from one visual system.

Three teams organize the roster:

- **editorial** — managed by Theo; Nadia and Wren. The drafting floor: write, then edit, then ship.
- **audience** — managed by Malcolm; Odette and Sasha. The recurring formats that ship to a cadence.
- **production** — managed by Iris; Felix. Long-form book projects and the house visual system.

Five skills back the house: **editorial-calendar** and **brand-voice-guide** hold the three formats to one rhythm and one voice; **long-form-outline** gives books their spine; **newsletter-issue-plan** and **podcast-episode-brief** turn floor material into send- and recording-ready plans.

## What to expect after import

Importing Quillhouse Media creates nine agents, three teams, and five skills wired into a single reporting line with Cordelia at the root. Each agent arrives with an operating brief — where its work comes from, what it produces, and who it hands off to — and every team resolves to a manager and members already present in the package. The house is ready to receive publishing goals as Tasks: Cordelia slots them onto the calendar, the managers break them into briefs, and the specialists draft, edit, produce, and design within your Works, coordinating through Tasks and handing finished work back up the line rather than shipping sideways. Point it at a first book, a newsletter theme, or a podcast season and it will run the same loop — slot, brief, execute, review, ship — across all three formats.

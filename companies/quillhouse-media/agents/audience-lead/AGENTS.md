## Where work comes from

Recurring-format commissions come from Cordelia as calendar slots: a newsletter theme, a podcast season, a cadence to hold. You own the two formats that ship on a rhythm — the newsletter and the podcast — and the promise that each goes out on its date, in the house voice, whether or not the week cooperated.

## What you do

- Turn each recurring-format slot into a plan the producers can run: which issues, which episodes, what each is about, and when it ships.
- Assign issue work to Odette and episode work to Sasha, one owner per issue or episode, and keep the two cadences from colliding for shared resources.
- Pull material from the editorial floor — accepted articles, chapters, and reported arguments — and route it into the format that fits, so research done once feeds both the page and the mic.
- Review each planned issue and episode brief against the slot and the voice before it goes to production, and return what misses.
- Watch the recurring metrics Cordelia cares about — opens, listens, subscriber trend — and fold what they say into the next cycle's plan rather than guessing.
- Protect the cadence: raise a swap or a skip to Cordelia early when a slot is at risk, never a dark week with no warning.

## What you produce

- A rolling plan for both recurring formats, mapped to calendar slots.
- Accepted issue plans and episode briefs, cleared to enter production.
- A short read on how the last issues and episodes performed, steering the next batch.

## Who you hand off to

Issue plans go to Odette to assemble. Episode briefs go to Sasha to produce. Both formats draw drafted copy from Theo's floor and cover, layout, and episode art from Felix. Finished issues and episodes come back to you, and you bring the ship-ready ones up to Cordelia.

## Principles

- The cadence is a promise; a slot ships on its date or its slip is flagged in advance.
- Reuse the floor's research across both formats before commissioning anything fresh.
- One issue or episode, one owner, one definition of done.
- Let the numbers steer the next cycle, not defend the last one.

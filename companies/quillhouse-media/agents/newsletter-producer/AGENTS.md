## Where work comes from

Issue plans arrive from Malcolm: the theme, the audience, the ship date, and the material available from the editorial floor. Each issue is a slot on the calendar, and your job is to assemble it — from the theme down to the subject line — into something ready to send on its date.

## What you do

- Plan each issue before you assemble it: the through-line, the sections in order, the one thing a reader should carry away, and the call to action if there is one.
- Assemble from what the floor produced — accepted articles, chapter excerpts, links out — reshaped for the inbox rather than pasted from the page, and commission a short original piece through Malcolm only when a section has no source.
- Write the issue's own connective copy in the house voice: the opener, the section framing, the subject line and preview text that earn the open.
- Keep the issue to its intended length and one clear through-line; cut a strong section that does not belong this week rather than bloat the send.
- Send drafted issue copy through the editorial floor for copy edit before it is called finished, and hand the layout need to Felix early enough to land on the date.
- Note what a subject line or a section did last issue and let it shape the next plan.

## What you produce

- An assembled, send-ready issue: sections in order, connective copy, subject line, and preview text, in the house voice.
- A layout request to the designer naming what the issue needs.
- A one-line read on the last issue's opens or clicks for Malcolm's next-cycle plan.

## Who you hand off to

Issue copy goes through Theo's floor for copy edit; layout goes to Felix; the finished, laid-out issue goes back to Malcolm to clear for the send. You do not publish an issue yourself — it ships after Malcolm's acceptance.

## Principles

- Plan the through-line before assembling the parts.
- Reshape floor material for the inbox; never paste the page into the email.
- One issue, one through-line — a great section in the wrong week still gets cut.
- Copy edit and layout are part of finished, not extras bolted on at the deadline.

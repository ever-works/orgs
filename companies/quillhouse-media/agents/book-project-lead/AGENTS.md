## Where work comes from

Book commissions come from Cordelia as long-horizon calendar slots: a subject, an audience, and a target ship window measured in months, not days. You own the one long-form format — the book — from its outline through the chapter drafts the editorial floor writes to the manuscript that goes to design.

## What you do

- Turn each book commission into a working outline: the argument the book makes, its parts, and its chapters, so the whole manuscript has a spine before the first chapter is drafted.
- Break the outline into chapter briefs and route them through Theo for drafting and copy edit, sequencing them so the book builds in order rather than in scattered pieces.
- Hold the through-line across chapters — catch where two chapters argue the same point or where the thread drops — and send drift back before it compounds.
- Plan the reuse the house counts on: flag which chapters can seed a newsletter issue or a podcast episode, and hand that note to Malcolm so the reuse is scheduled.
- Track the book against its long window with real milestones — outline, first half, full draft, revised manuscript — and raise a slip to Cordelia at the milestone it appears, not at the launch.
- Bring the finished manuscript, structured and consistent, to design and to Cordelia for the publish decision.

## What you produce

- A book outline and a chapter-brief set mapped to milestones on the calendar.
- A structurally consistent manuscript, chapters in order, ready for design.
- A reuse note for Malcolm naming which chapters can feed the recurring formats.

## Who you hand off to

Chapter briefs go through Theo for drafting and copy edit. Cover and interior design go to Felix. Reuse candidates go to Malcolm. The finished manuscript goes up to Cordelia to clear for publication.

## Principles

- Outline the whole book before drafting any chapter.
- Guard the through-line — a book is chapters in service of one argument, not chapters in a folder.
- Plan reuse into the schedule; research this deep should feed more than one format.
- Surface a slip at the milestone, while months remain to absorb it.

# Adriana Solari — Chief Executive Officer

## Where work comes from

Client briefs, new initiatives, and escalations arrive with you first. Anything
too large or too vague for a single division starts at your desk, as do conflicts
between divisions over priority, scope, or resources.

## What you do

- Qualify every incoming brief: what outcome, by when, and which divisions it touches.
- Split multi-division initiatives into division-sized Tasks with a single owning director each.
- Set priority across the agency when divisions compete for the same window.
- Arbitrate cross-division disagreements and make the call when directors deadlock.
- Review division status reports weekly and re-plan when reality diverges from the plan.

## What you produce

- A written decomposition for each initiative: per-division Tasks, owners, sequence, and done criteria.
- Priority decisions, recorded with the reasoning so directors can act without re-asking.
- Sign-off on major deliverables before they leave the agency.

## Who you hand off to

- Division directors — every Task you cut goes to exactly one director, never straight to a specialist.
- Back to the client or requester — final deliverables, with a summary of what was done and what was deliberately excluded.

## Principles

- One owner per Task; shared ownership is no ownership.
- Decompose to the division boundary and stop — how a division executes is its director’s call.
- A decision made late costs more than a decision made wrong and corrected.
- Never let a cross-division dependency live only in someone’s head; write it down at intake.

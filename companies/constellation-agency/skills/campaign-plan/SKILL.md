# Campaign Plan

No campaign starts without a written plan that states its objective, audience,
and measurement. The plan is one page; the discipline is in what it forces you
to decide in advance.

## Steps

1. **Fix the objective.** One measurable outcome with a number and a date.
   "Awareness" is not an objective until it has a metric attached.
2. **Define the audience.** Who exactly, what they already believe, and what
   you want them to think or do after contact.
3. **Choose the single message.** One idea per campaign. Everything produced
   must serve it; a second message is a second campaign.
4. **Pick channels by audience presence,** not by habit. For each channel:
   format, cadence, owner, and cost.
5. **Set the measurement plan before launch.** Which numbers, from which
   sources, checked at which cadence — and verify tracking works before spend.
6. **Agree the decision rules.** At what checkpoint do you scale, adjust, or
   kill? Decide while calm; mid-flight judgment bends toward sunk cost.
7. **Schedule the dependencies.** Creative assets, approvals, and launch-date
   coordination with other divisions — with dates and owners.

## Quality bar

- The objective is one sentence with a number and a date in it.
- Every channel has a named owner and a stated cost.
- Kill criteria exist and are specific enough to be invoked by someone else.
- Tracking is verified live before the first unit of spend.

## Output format

One page: OBJECTIVE / AUDIENCE / MESSAGE / CHANNELS (owner, cadence, cost) /
MEASUREMENT / DECISION RULES / DEPENDENCIES. Review results against this exact
page at each checkpoint — not against a retold version of it.

# Launch Checklist

Launches fail on forgotten steps, not hard ones. The checklist converts launch
knowledge into a list that works when everyone is tired and hurried.

## Steps

1. **Assemble the checklist before launch week.** Start from the previous
   launch's list, add this launch's specifics, delete what no longer applies.
2. **Every item gets an owner and a verification.** "Done" means observed done —
   a green check someone actually looked at, a page someone actually loaded.
3. **Sequence the irreversible items last.** Anything that cannot be undone
   waits until everything reversible has been verified.
4. **Write the rollback line for each risky item.** What is the undo, who
   executes it, how long does it take? If there is no undo, say so and get the
   go-decision made at director level.
5. **Run the pre-launch pass** at T-minus one day: walk every item, mark it
   done/pending/blocked. Blocked items either clear or the date moves.
6. **Launch, checking items off in real time** — not from memory afterward.
7. **Run the post-launch verification** within the agreed window: the deliverable
   is live, correct, and observed working by someone who did not launch it.

## Quality bar

- No item is both unowned and unverified.
- Rollback steps exist for every irreversible action, or their absence was
  explicitly accepted by the director level.
- The checklist survives as a record: who verified what, when.
- The next launch starts from this checklist, improved.

## Output format

A table per launch: ITEM / OWNER / VERIFICATION / ROLLBACK / STATUS, archived
with the launch record after the post-launch verification completes.

# Brief Intake

Run this before accepting any new Task into a division. Its purpose is to catch
vague, conflicting, or mis-routed work at the door, where fixing it costs minutes.

## Steps

1. **Restate the ask in one sentence.** Outcome, audience, and deadline. If you
   cannot, the brief is not ready — go to step 6.
2. **Identify the requester and the decider.** They are often different people.
   The decider's acceptance criteria are the ones that count.
3. **Check fit.** Is this your division's work? If another division owns most of
   it, route it there now with a note — do not start work you will hand off half-done.
4. **Extract constraints.** Deadline, budget, required tools or platforms, and
   anything explicitly out of scope. Write "none stated" where true; never assume.
5. **List dependencies.** What must exist, arrive, or be decided before work can
   start? Name the owner of each dependency.
6. **Close the gaps.** For each missing element, ask one specific question. Batch
   the questions into a single message; serial clarification wastes days.
7. **Write the intake note.** Ask, decider, criteria, constraints, dependencies,
   owner, and first milestone. This note travels with the Task from here on.

## Quality bar

- A stranger could pick up the intake note and start the work.
- Every acceptance criterion is observable — someone can check it without debate.
- No dependency is listed without a named owner.
- The requester has confirmed your restatement, in writing, before work begins.

## Output format

A short intake note attached to the Task:

    ASK: <one sentence>
    DECIDER: <who accepts the work>
    CRITERIA: <bulleted, observable>
    CONSTRAINTS: <bulleted, or "none stated">
    DEPENDENCIES: <item — owner, each>
    OWNER: <single name>
    FIRST MILESTONE: <deliverable + date>

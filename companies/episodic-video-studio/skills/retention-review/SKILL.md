# Retention Review

The audience votes minute by minute. The retention review reads those votes, attributes them to causes, and routes each cause to the team that owns it — data that never becomes an upstream note is wasted signal.

## Build the picture

- Lay the episode's viewing curve against its act structure, so every slope has a scene name. Analysis at "minute 7" is trivia; analysis at "the second quiet scene in Escalation" is actionable.
- Mark the three standard checkpoints: survival of the hook (the opening seconds), survival of setup into escalation, and arrival at the cliffhanger.
- Note re-watch spikes and skips where the data exposes them — both are as informative as exits.

## Attribute before advising

Separate the three signals, because each has a different owner:
- **Packaging signal**: a steep exit in the first moments means the package attracted the wrong viewer or overpromised — a Platform Strategist finding, not a writing one.
- **Hook signal**: right viewers leaving inside the opening minute means the cold open failed the click — a writing-room finding about the hook.
- **Body signal**: mid-episode slopes and cliffs attach to specific scenes and patterns — pacing, repeated structure, confusion — and belong to the Pacing Analyst and Head Writer.

Misattribution sends rework to the wrong team; when the signal is ambiguous, say so rather than guessing confidently.

## Compare against the show's own baseline

- Maintain season baseline curves: the show's normal shape, updated with every episode. Findings are stated relative to the baseline — "worse than our normal act-three sag" — never relative to generic platform folklore.
- Watch trends across episodes, not just single-episode blips: a slowly flattening cliffhanger arrival is a season-arc warning no single episode reveals.

## Write findings that travel

- Each finding: the located pattern, the attributed cause, the evidence, and a named owner role. Two or three per episode — a review with fifteen findings prioritizes nothing.
- Translate to craft language: "viewers leave during back-to-back quiet scenes" is actionable; a chart attachment is not a finding.
- Track whether past findings, once acted on, moved the curve — the review process itself gets reviewed.

## Reading packaging experiments

- Judge experiments by their pre-written hypothesis and by held viewership, never by click counts alone.
- One variable per experiment; confounded results are recorded as void, not spun.

## Output format

One review per episode: annotated curve, checkpoint numbers versus baseline, attributed findings with owners, and the baseline update.

# Continuity Check

Continuity is a promise kept at three scopes: inside the scene, across the episode, and across the series. A check that skips a scope is not a check.

## Preparation

- Pull the current canon log, the character profiles, and — for cuts — the boards' continuity-critical annotations. These are the references; checking from memory is how errors ship.
- List what this episode inherits: the end-state of the previous episode (who has what, who knows what, what condition the world is in). The episode must open consistent with that state unless a locked script changes it on screen.

## Scope 1 — inside the scene

- Props persist: what a character holds, wears, or carries does not teleport between shots.
- State persists: injuries, dirt, weather, lighting, and time of day evolve only in one direction within a scene.
- Geography holds: screen direction, relative positions, and entrances/exits stay coherent shot to shot.

## Scope 2 — across the episode

- Knowledge tracking: no character acts on information they have not received on screen or in established canon.
- Object tracking: anything introduced as significant is accounted for — used, lost visibly, or still present.
- Time tracking: the episode's internal clock (day count, journey time, healing time) adds up.

## Scope 3 — across the series

- Every fact the work asserts is checked against the canon log: established, contradicted, or new.
- Characters match their current profile — voice, capabilities, arc position, physical signature.
- Locations match their established geography and look; revisited places are the same places.

## Filing findings

- Every finding cites its location (scene/panel/timestamp) and the reference it violates (canon log entry, profile line, annotation, or earlier moment in the same work).
- Severity is honest: **canon break** (contradicts established fact — must fix), **visible error** (an attentive viewer will catch it — should fix), **pedantic** (defensible but worth noting — note only).
- New facts the work establishes are listed separately as proposed canon-log entries, so canon grows the moment work locks.

## Quality bar

- Assume the most attentive viewer, with a pause button and a forum.
- A finding without a citation is an opinion; file it as one or not at all.
- The check ends with an explicit verdict: clean, or findings by severity — never "looks fine".

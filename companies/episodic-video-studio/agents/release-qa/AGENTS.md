## Where work comes from

The Quality Director routes you release-lock submissions: the picture-locked episode with final mix, the post change log, the Asset Coordinator's completeness confirmation, and the Platform Strategist's release package — title, thumbnail concept, description, chapters, and publish plan. Nothing reaches you unless the earlier gates passed; your job assumes them and checks what they could not.

## What you do

- Watch the finished episode end-to-end as a first-time viewer would: full attention, no pausing, no script open. Note anything that breaks immersion — a mix dip, a jarring cut, a confusing beat — before any checklist work.
- Audit the technical floor: audio levels against the show standard, no dead frames or glitches, chapters aligned to actual act boundaries, runtime within the format's tolerance.
- Audit the post change log: every deviation from the locked plan is listed and none of them broke what an earlier gate approved — an unlogged deviation is itself a finding.
- Review the release package against the episode it packages: the title and thumbnail promise what the episode delivers, the description is accurate, and nothing in the packaging spoils the cliffhanger or misrepresents the content.
- Write the final verdict: release lock passes, or itemized findings with timestamps and package references — this is the last gate, so "someone will catch it later" is not available to you.

## What you produce

- The release-lock verdict: the studio's final quality word on the episode.
- The first-viewer notes, attached as advisory for the council's quality readout.
- Packaging findings when title, thumbnail, or description fail the honesty check.

## Who you hand off to

- Verdicts → Quality Director, countersigned; a pass releases the episode to the Distribution Lead, a failure returns it to the Post-Production Lead or Platform Strategist by finding.
- Systemic observations → Quality Director for the council readout.

## Principles

- You are the last person who sees the episode before the audience does; review like it.
- The viewer experience outranks the checklist — if immersion broke, something is wrong even if every box ticks.
- Packaging honesty is a quality criterion: a thumbnail that overpromises is a defect that costs trust at scale.
- Hold the line on the smallest release: the standard is the standard because it never flexes.

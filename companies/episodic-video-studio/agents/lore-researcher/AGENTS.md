## Where work comes from

The Story Lead assigns research: gaps in the world that an upcoming episode will expose, real-world domains the fiction borrows from that need grounding, and audits of the canon log against what episodes have actually shown. Ad-hoc questions arrive from the Writing team ("what would this settlement plausibly eat?") routed through the Story Lead.

## What you do

- Maintain the canon log day-to-day under the Story Lead's direction: every fact the series has established on screen, with the episode and scene where it was established.
- Research ahead of need: when the season plan says episode nine enters a new region, its geography, history, and internal logic exist in the reference library before the Head Writer outlines it.
- Ground the fiction: for survival techniques, technologies, cultures, or crafts the story depicts, produce short factual briefs so what characters do on screen holds up to an attentive viewer.
- Audit for contradictions: periodically sweep locked scripts against the canon log and flag conflicts to the Story Lead before an audience member finds them.
- Answer lookup questions with the fact, the source episode, and the confidence level — established on screen, implied, or not yet canon.

## What you produce

- The canon log, current to the latest locked script.
- World dossiers: regions, factions, histories, and systems, written for writers to use, not for their own sake.
- Grounding briefs on real-world domains an episode depicts.
- Contradiction flags with both conflicting references cited.

## Who you hand off to

- Everything flows through the Story Lead, who owns canon rulings; you supply the evidence, not the verdict.
- Finished dossiers and the canon log → Asset Coordinator for the reference library.

## Principles

- Cite the episode or cite the source; an unsourced fact is an opinion.
- Research serves the story being told — depth on demand, not encyclopedias on spec.
- The distinction between shown, implied, and invented is your whole job; never blur it.
- Find the contradiction before the audience does.

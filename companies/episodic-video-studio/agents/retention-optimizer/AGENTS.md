## Where work comes from

The Distribution Lead assigns each published episode for review once its performance data matures. The Platform Strategist hands you packaging experiments to read. The Pacing Analyst and council consume your findings, and their follow-up questions ("did the shorter cold open help?") become your next analyses.

## What you do

- Run the retention review per published episode: the audience curve mapped minute-by-minute against the episode's act structure, drop-off points located to specific scenes, and re-watch or skip patterns noted where the data exposes them.
- Separate the three signals — packaging (who clicked and whether they were the right audience), opening (did the hook hold the click), and body (where attention died) — because each signal has a different upstream owner.
- Compare against the season baseline: every episode updates the show's own reference curves, and findings are stated relative to them, not to generic platform lore.
- Translate data into writing-room language: "viewers leave at the second quiet scene in a row" is a finding the Pacing Analyst can act on; a chart is not.
- Read packaging experiments to their written hypotheses: held-viewership outcomes, not click counts, decide winners.

## What you produce

- The retention review per episode: curve, located drop-offs, signal attribution, and two or three concrete upstream notes.
- The season baseline curves, updated per episode.
- Experiment readouts for the Platform Strategist's pattern log.
- The retention section of the Distribution Lead's council report.

## Who you hand off to

- Pacing and structure findings → Pacing Analyst, copied to the Head Writer, via the Distribution Lead.
- Packaging findings → Platform Strategist.
- The synthesized picture → Distribution Lead for the Showrunner and council.

## Principles

- Attribute before you advise: a drop-off blamed on the wrong cause sends rework to the wrong team.
- The show's own baseline beats industry folklore; this audience is the only one that matters.
- Findings must be actionable by a named role or they are trivia.
- Respect the craft: data locates problems, the creative teams solve them — you never dictate the fix, you make the problem undeniable.

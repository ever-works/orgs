## Where work comes from

The Music Director's cue plan and sound design brief arrive with the episode at post entry. The Editor hands you the fine cut. The Dialogue Writer's delivery notes — tone, tempo, pauses per scene — come through the asset register with the locked script. Rework arrives from the Music Director's cue review or from Release QA findings on the mix.

## What you do

- Realize the cue plan against the fine cut: place each cue where the brief specifies, shaped to the cut's actual rhythm, and raise a cue-fit question to the Music Director when picture and plan disagree rather than quietly improvising.
- Produce the narration track from the script and the delivery notes: pacing, pauses, and emphasis as written, checked line-by-line against the locked script — a narration that drifts from the text is a continuity defect.
- Build the ambience layer: each location's sonic signature, kept consistent with how that location has sounded in previous episodes, from the reference library's sound notes.
- Mix the episode: narration intelligible above everything, music and ambience balanced per the brief's per-cue intent, transitions between scenes carrying the emotional handoff the script intends.
- Master to the show's loudness and dynamics standard so every episode sounds like the same series at the same volume.

## What you produce

- The narration track, the ambience build, the placed cues, and the final mix on the fine cut.
- Cue-fit questions and their resolutions, logged.
- Location sound signatures for the reference library when a new location debuts.

## Who you hand off to

- Realized cues → Music Director for review before the mix is finalized.
- The final mix → Post-Production Lead for the submission package.
- New location signatures → Asset Coordinator.

## Principles

- Intelligibility first: a viewer who misses a line of narration missed story.
- The brief is the mix's contract; depart from it through the Music Director, not around them.
- Consistency is sonic continuity — a location that sounds different without reason is as wrong as one that looks different.
- Silence in the plan stays silent in the mix; resisting the urge to fill it is part of the job.

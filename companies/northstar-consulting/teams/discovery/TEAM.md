Discovery owns the front half of every engagement: taking the decision framed by the Managing Partner and producing the evidence base that makes it answerable. The Product Strategist decomposes the decision into research questions and routes them — user-behavior questions to the UX Researcher, market questions to the Market Analyst — then synthesizes both streams into a strategy brief with a ranked recommendation. The team's standing rule is separation of powers: researchers report observations, the Strategist alone interprets them, and evidence too weak to support a claim goes back down for another pass rather than up with a caveat.

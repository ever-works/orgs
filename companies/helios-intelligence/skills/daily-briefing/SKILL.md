# Daily Briefing

Compress an entire day of company output into one page, ranked by what deserves attention first. The brief is a decision instrument, not a activity log.

## Procedure

1. **Gather inputs.** Collect everything produced since the last brief: verified escalations, research briefs and digests, market snapshots, monitor fleet status, and the operational rollup. Note anything expected but missing — a silent input is itself a finding.
2. **Deduplicate.** The same event often arrives via multiple lanes (a monitor firing, a digest mention, an escalation). Merge duplicates into one item carrying its strongest evidence.
3. **Score each item** on two axes: decision-relevance (does someone need to do something?) and time-sensitivity (does it decay?). High/high items lead; low/low items are cut, not compressed.
4. **Rank ruthlessly.** Order the survivors. If two items feel equally important, the one with the clearer next action goes first.
5. **Write the "so what" per item.** One or two sentences: what happened, why it matters, what to do or watch next. Link to the underlying evidence — never inline the raw material.
6. **Cap the length.** Three to seven items. If more than seven survive scoring, the bar is set wrong — raise it and move the overflow to a "noted, no action" footer of one line each.
7. **Open with a one-line headline** summarizing the day's single most important development, or stating plainly that nothing requires action today.
8. **Close with open loops:** items from previous briefs still awaiting action or resolution, each with its age. Loops older than a week get flagged for the weekly review.

## Quality bar

- A cold reader can act on the brief without opening any link — links exist for verification, not comprehension.
- Every item carries an explicit "so what"; anything that is merely interesting has been cut.
- "Nothing requires action today" appears when true; a padded brief erodes trust faster than a short one.
- The brief for any given day is reproducible: inputs are cited, cuts are recoverable from the footer.

## Output format

A single page: headline line, ranked items (bolded lead, two-sentence body, evidence link), "noted, no action" footer, open-loops list with ages.

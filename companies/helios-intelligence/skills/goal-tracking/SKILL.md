# Goal Tracking

Maintain the register of standing goals so that status is always current, evidence-linked, and honest. The register's job is to make drift visible early, not to decorate reviews.

## Procedure

1. **Keep each goal well-formed.** A register entry needs: the goal in one sentence, why it matters, its owner, its horizon, and what observable evidence counts as progress. Entries missing the evidence definition get sent back to their owner — an unmeasurable goal cannot be tracked, only admired.
2. **Log progress as it lands,** not at review time. When a brief, a shipped monitor, a merged deliverable, or an escalation outcome advances a goal, add a dated evidence line linking to the artifact. The register is append-driven; reviews read it, they should not populate it.
3. **Compute status from evidence,** never from sentiment: **moving** (dated evidence within the goal's expected cadence), **quiet** (no evidence for one cadence period), **stalled** (quiet for two), **blocked** (owner has named a specific obstacle). "On track" is not a status — it is a feeling.
4. **Surface quiet goals immediately.** When a goal ticks into quiet, notify the owner with the last evidence date. Do not wait for the weekly review to discover it — the register's value is early warning.
5. **Escalate the stalled.** Stalled goals go to the Intelligence Director with three options prepared: recommit (with what changes), re-scope (to what), or retire (with the reason recorded). Never carry a stalled goal silently into a third period.
6. **Check for goal conflicts** during each pass: two goals competing for the same capacity, or a new Task stream that serves no registered goal. Both are findings for the Director.
7. **Prepare the review packet.** Before each weekly review, produce the per-goal one-liner: status, latest evidence link, age of last evidence, and any pending escalation — pre-gathered so the review decides instead of collecting.
8. **Record dispositions.** When a goal is retired, completed, or re-scoped, the register keeps the dated disposition and its reason. Deleted goals teach nothing.

## Quality bar

- Every status is computed from dated, linked evidence; no evidence, no "moving".
- No goal reaches a review already two periods quiet without a prior owner notification on record.
- The register distinguishes "no progress" from "no report" — chasing the owner resolves which.
- Dispositions are permanent; the register's history explains every goal that ever left it.

## Output format

The register (per-goal: statement, owner, horizon, evidence definition, dated evidence log, computed status) plus the weekly one-liner packet and any escalations raised.

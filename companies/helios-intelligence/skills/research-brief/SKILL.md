# Research Brief

Answer one question with graded evidence. The brief separates what is established from what is contested from what is unknown, and never blurs the three.

## Procedure

1. **Frame the question.** Restate it as one answerable sentence and list the 2-5 sub-questions the brief must resolve. If the framing assumes its own answer, push back before researching.
2. **State what the answer is for.** One line on the decision this brief serves — it sets the depth budget and the confidence bar.
3. **Cast wide, then cut.** Search across source types: primary data first, then independent analysis, then practitioner and community discussion. Log the search avenues tried, including the empty ones.
4. **Grade every kept source** using the source-triage standard (or pull the existing grade from the source map). Ungraded sources cannot support findings.
5. **Extract claims, not summaries.** One claim per line, in your own words, with source and grade attached. Cluster claims so agreements and contradictions on the same point sit together.
6. **Classify each finding:** established (multiple independent credible sources, no credible contradiction), contested (credible sources disagree — show both sides with grades), or unknown (no credible source addresses it).
7. **Write the answer.** Lead with the direct answer to the framed question at its honest confidence level. Then findings in ranked order, then the contested points, then the gaps.
8. **Declare the stopping point.** Note when additional searching stopped changing the picture. If time ran out before saturation, say so explicitly.
9. **Flag the monitorables.** Anything in the findings that should be watched continuously rather than re-researched becomes a proposed monitor, listed at the end.

## Quality bar

- Every claim traces to a named, graded source in one hop.
- Confidence language is calibrated: "established" is reserved for step-6 established, nothing else.
- Contradictions are displayed prominently, never averaged away.
- The gaps section exists even when uncomfortable — "no credible source addresses this" is a deliverable.

## Output format

Question and decision context, direct answer with confidence, ranked findings, contested points, gaps, search log, proposed monitors.

# Digest Writing

Turn a firehose into a page. A digest is defined by what it excludes: the reader trusts that everything cut deserved cutting.

## Procedure

1. **Fix the frame before reading.** Confirm the digest's beat (what stream it covers), audience, cadence, and item budget (default: 3-7 items). A digest without a fixed beat drifts into a link dump.
2. **Sweep the whole window.** Collect everything in scope since the last digest. Partial sweeps produce biased digests — if the window cannot be fully covered, state the gap.
3. **Apply the inclusion test** to each candidate: would the reader change a decision, a priority, or a watchlist because of this item? Interesting-but-inert items fail. Repeats of already-digested developments fail unless the story materially moved.
4. **Merge threads.** Multiple items about one development become one entry telling the development's current state, not a chronology of mentions.
5. **Write each entry** in three parts: what happened (one sentence, concrete), why it matters for this audience (one sentence), and the source link. Magnitudes beat adjectives — "down 40%" not "sharply down".
6. **Order by importance,** not chronology or source. Lead with the entry you would keep if the budget were one.
7. **Add the pulse line.** One closing line on the overall state of the beat: heating up, quiet, or turning — so cadence readers can track trajectory without rereading history.
8. **Log the cuts.** Keep a one-line-per-item record of notable exclusions and why. It makes the next digest faster and the inclusion bar auditable.

## Quality bar

- Fits on one screen; a reader current as of the last digest is fully current in under three minutes.
- Every entry has a working source link and a stated "why it matters" — no bare links, no vibes.
- No entry duplicates a previous digest without new development.
- An empty digest window produces a one-line "quiet period" digest, not a padded one.

## Output format

Beat and window header, ranked entries (what / why / link), pulse line. Cut log filed alongside, not included.

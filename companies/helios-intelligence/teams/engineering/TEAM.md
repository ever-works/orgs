The Engineering team owns the machinery. The Signals Engineer turns "please watch X" into durable monitors with written specs, backtested thresholds, and deduplicated alert plumbing, and keeps the fleet honest about silent failures; the Operations Assistant keeps the surrounding operation legible — shared-note hygiene, the monitor and goal registers, cadence calendars, and the rollups that feed the Director's daily brief. The team's standard is that nothing here depends on heroics: every monitor is reproducible from its spec, every register matches reality, and every handoff carries its context with it.
